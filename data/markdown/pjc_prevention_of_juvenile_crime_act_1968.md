An Act to provide the means of prevention of the commission of crimes by children and
to provide generally for the control of children and for the protection of indigent,
neglected or delinquent children.
BE IT ENACTED by the Legislative Assembly or the Cook Islands in Session
assembled, and by the authority or the same, as follows:
1 Short title, commencement and division into Parts
(1) This Act may be cited as the Prevention of Juvenile Crime Act 1968.
(2) This Act comes into force in any island on the date to be appointed by the High
Commissioner by Order in Executive Council and dif ferent dates may be so
appointed in respect of dif ferent islands.
(3) This Act is divided into Parts, as follows:
Part 1 — Administration (section 3)
Part 2 — Prevention of juvenile wrong-doing (sections 4 to 18)
Part 3 — Children’s Courts (sections 19 to 38)
Part 4 — Appeals (section 39)
Part 5 — Miscellaneous (sections 40 to 43)

21 Proceedings specially af fecting children

Provided that where a person other than a child is charged with the commission
of an of fence against a child such charge must be heard and determined in the

(2) No proceedings relating to a charge of murder or manslaughter brought against
a child or any other person may be heard in the Children’s Court but all such

other Court ordinarily exercises jurisdiction, nor may a sitting of the Children’s

40 No Court fees payable
No Court fees are payable in respect of proceedings under this Act.
41 In absence of evidence Court may ﬁx age of child
If in any judicial proceedings under this Act the age of any child or young

are consistent with this Act provided that in such rules, and in so far as they do
not extend, the practice and procedure of the Children’s Court is such as the
Court thinks in each case to be most consistent with natural justice and
conviction.
