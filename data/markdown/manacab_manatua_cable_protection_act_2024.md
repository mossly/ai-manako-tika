An Act to help protect the Manatua One Polynesian cable.
The Parliament of the Cook Islands enacts as follows—
1 Title
This Act is the Manatua Cable Protection Act 2024.
2 Commencement
This Act comes into force on the day after the date it is assented to by the King’s
Representative.

Anchor exclusion zones
5 Anchor exclusion zones
The areas described in the Schedule are anchor exclusion zones for the purposes
of this Act.
6 Of fence to anchor vessel in anchor exclusion zone
(1) A vessel must not be anchored in an anchor exclusion zone.
(2) The owner and the master of a vessel that is anchored in breach of subsection (1)
each commit an of fence (even if the vessel is outside the exclusion zone while
the anchor is in the exclusion zone).
(3) A person who commits an of fence under subsection (2) is liable on conviction—
(a) to a ﬁne not exceeding $100,000 if—
(i) the of fence was committed in the course of conducting an activity
for the purpose of producing a commercial gain; or

7 Of fence to anchor object in anchor exclusion zone
(1) An object must not be anchored in an anchor exclusion zone.
(2) The person who owns, and the person who is in control of, an object that is
anchored in breach of subsection (1) each commit an of fence (even if the object
is outside the exclusion zone while the anchor is in the exclusion zone).
(3) A person who commits an of fence under subsection (2) is liable on conviction—

(i) the of fence was committed in the course of conducting an activity
for the purpose of producing a commercial gain; or

commission of the of fence:
(b) to a ﬁne not exceeding $20,000 if the of fence was committed, otherwise
than in any of the circumstances speciﬁed in paragraph (a).
8 Defence in respect of anchor exclusion zone of fences
It is a defence to a prosecution for an of fence against section 6(2) or 7(2) if the
defendant proves that—
(a) the anchoring that is alleged to constitute the of fence was necessary for
the purpose of saving a life or a vessel; or
(b) all reasonable steps to prevent the commission of the of fence had been
taken.
9 V essel may be ordered to remove anchor from anchor exclusion zone
(1) If an enforcement of f icer believes on reasonable grounds that a vessel is
anchored in an anchor exclusion zone, the enforcement of f icer may , by any
means of communication, order the master of the vessel to remove the anchor
from the exclusion zone.
(2) A master who, without reasonable excuse, fails within a reasonable time period
to comply with an order given under this section commits an of fence.
(3) A person who commits an of fence under subsection (2) is liable on conviction
to a ﬁne not exceeding $10,000.
(4) This section does not limit any other provision of this Act.

11 Secretary may impose conditions on anchoring for maintenance activities
(1) The Secretary may impose conditions on the anchoring of a vessel or object that

(2) The Secretary must provide the conditions in writing to the cable operator.
(3) The cable operator must ensure that the conditions are complied with.
(4) The cable operator commits an of fence if it breaches subsection (3) and is liable

(5) It is a defence to a prosecution for an of fence against subsection (4) if the cable
operator proves that it took all reasonable steps to ensure that the conditions
were complied with.
12 Anchor exclusion zone signage
(1) The cable operator must, to the extent practicable, ensure that each anchor
exclusion zone is signposted where the cable comes ashore.
(2) The cable operator must ensure that the signs state—
(a) where the exclusion zone begins and ends; and
(b) what activities are prohibited within the exclusion zone.
(3) It is the responsibility of the cable operator to erect and maintain the signs at the
operator’s expense.

Part 2
Damage to cable and cable incidents
13 Damage to cable
(1) A person who intentionally , recklessly , or negligently damages the cable
(whether or not a vessel or object is involved in causing the damage) or causes
or permits a vessel or equipment belonging to a vessel to damage the cable,
commits an of fence.
(2) The owner or master of a vessel that is used in the commission of an of fence
against subsection (1) commits an of fence.
(3) A person who commits an of fence under subsection (1) or (2) is liable on
conviction to a ﬁne not exceeding $250,000.
(4) An owner or master of a vessel who is convicted of an of fence against
subsection (1) is not liable for an of fence against subsection (2) arising out of
the same course of conduct.
(5) A person commits an of fence under this section whether the damage or the act
or omission causing the damage occurs inside or outside an anchor exclusion
zone.
14 Defence in respect of cable damage of fence
It is a defence to a prosecution for an of fence against section 13 if the defendant
proves that the damage that is alleged to constitute the of fence was caused by
persons acting with the sole object of saving a life or a vessel after having taken
all reasonable precautions to avoid the damage.
15 Reporting of cable incident
(1) An owner of a vessel that has a cable incident must notify the Secretary of the
incident, as soon as practicable, in a form approved by the Secretary .
(2) A person who has a cable incident other than one described in subsection (1)
must notify the Secretary , as soon as practicable, in a form approved by the
Secretary .
(3) A person who, without reasonable excuse, fails to notify a cable incident as
required by subsections (1) and (2) commits an of fence and is liable on
conviction to a ﬁne not exceeding $20,000.
(4) In this section, cable incident means an occurrence in which a person or a
vessel, object, or other thing (including an anchor) hits or damages the cable.
16 Investigation of cable incidents by Secretary
Where a cable incident is required to be notiﬁed to the Secretary under
section 15, the Secretary may investigate the incident in accordance with
sections 40 to 42 of the Maritime Transport Act 2008.

Part 3
Other provisions
17 Navigational aids
(1) The cable operator must, at their own expense,—
(a) maintain all navigational aids designed or used to help vessels identify
where the cable is located; and
(b) replace the navigational aids and erect new aids if required by the
Secretary , at the operator’s expense.
(2) T o avoid doubt, section 94(7) and (8) of the Maritime Transport Act 2008
applies to the cable operator.
18 Act not to limit liability
Nothing in this Act limits or af fects the liability for damages of any person in
respect of any damage to the cable.
19 Other Acts not af fected
The provisions in this Act are in addition to, and not in substitution for, the
provisions of any other enactment and, except as expressly provided by this Act,
nothing in this Act derogates from the provisions of any other enactment.
