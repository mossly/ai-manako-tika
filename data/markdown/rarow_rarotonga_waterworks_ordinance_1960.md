An Ordinance of the Cook Islands to make provisions for the establishment maintenance
and control of waterworks in the Island of Rarotonga.

3 Minister may construct, extend, improve, or maintain waterworks
(1) By notice published in the Gazette the Minister may from time to time declare
that the water in any river, stream, lake, or pool is required for the purposes of
this Ordinance and that it is his intention to use the water therein for the public
supply in a manner to be speciﬁed in the notice.
(2) Within 90 days of the publication of any notice under the preceding subsection
any person may by notice addressed to the Minister lodge an objection to the use
of such river, stream, lake, or pool for the public supply and all such objections
shall be heard and determined in the High Court of the Cook Islands.
(3) In considering objections lodged in accordance with the preceding subsection
the High Court shall have regard to the public interest and may uphold or
dismiss the objection or may state conditions under which the Minister may use
the water in respect of which the notice has been given and any such
determination by the High Court shall be binding on the Minister so far as that
particular notice is concerned but shall not limit the right of the Minister to
make a similar declaration in respect of the same water at some later date.

6 Water supplies
(1) Every person who shall desire a domestic or a commercial supply of water shall
apply in writing to the Minister and if such application shall be approved, the
Minister shall cause such a supply to be laid on from the waterworks. Every
such person shall pay the expense of laying on such water-supply together with
the reasonable cost of pipes, taps, apparatus, and accessories supplied in
connection therewith. The Minister may require a deposit of money to be made
before the laying on of any water-supply .
(2) W ater supplied for household purposes only shall be deemed to be a “domestic”
supply .
(3) W ater supplied for other than household purposes shall be deemed to be a
“commercial” supply provided that in any case where, in the opinion of the

11 Savings
For all purposes whatsoever in respect of any fee, charge, or other sum of
money which at the commencement of this Ordinance has already been charged
or paid or is still chargeable or payable in respect of any period prior to the
coming into force of this Ordinance in accordance with any Ordinance or
enactment relating to the Rarotonga W aterworks in force prior to the
commencement of this Ordinance, and all the provisions thereof including its
penal provisions, shall be deemed to remain in full force and ef fect; and all
proceedings for the recovery of any ﬁne or penalty in respect of any of fence
committed whether before or after the commencement of this Ordinance may be
taken over or continued accordingly as if this Ordinance had not been passed.
