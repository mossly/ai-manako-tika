An Act to control the practice of dentistry .
BE IT ENACTED by the Parliament of the Cook Islands in Session assembled, and by
the authority of the same, as follows:
1 Short title
This Act may be cited as the Dental Act 1970–71.
2 Interpretation
In this Act unless the context otherwise requires,—
Department of Health [Repealed]
Ministry means the Ministry of Health
practice of dentistry includes—
(a) the performance of any operation and the treatment of any diseases,
deﬁciencies, or lesions, on or of the human teeth or jaws and the
correction of malpositions thereof and the performance of radiographic
work in connection with the human teeth or jaws:
(b) the giving of any anaesthetic in connection with any operation on the
human teeth or jaws:
(c) the mechanical construction or the renewal of artiﬁcial dentures or
restorative dental appliances:
(d) the performance of any operation on or the giving of any treatment,
advice, or attendance to any person as preparatory to, or for the purpose
of, or for or in connection with, the ﬁtting, insertion, ﬁxing, constructing,
repairing, or renewing of artiﬁcial dentures, or restorative dental
appliances:
(e) the performance of any such operation and the giving of any such
treatment advice, or attendance as is usually performed or given by
dentists,
and the expression to practise , in relation to dentistry , has a corresponding
meaning.
[Section 2 Department of Health: repealed, on 29 April 1974, by Schedule 2 of the Ministry of
Social Services Act 1973–74 (1973–74 No 35).]
[Section 2 Ministry: replaced, on 22 July 1996, by Schedule Part 2 of the Ministry of Health
Act 1995–96 (1995–96 No 6).]
3 Principal Dental Of ficer
(1) There must be appointed under the provisions of the Public Service Act 1969 a
Principal Dental Of f icer of the Dental Division of the Ministry of Health who,
subject to the control of the head of that Ministry and the Minister responsible
for that Ministry , is charged with the administration of the Dental Division of
that Ministry .
(2) The Principal Dental Of f icer must have the qualiﬁcation prescribed in
section 5(a) or (b).
[Section 3(1): amended, on 29 April 1974, by Schedule 2 of the Ministry of Social Services
Act 1973–74 (1973–74 No 35); on 27 December 1984, by Schedule Part 2 of the Ministry of Health
Act 1984 (1984 No 19).]

7 Charges for dental services
(1) Dental services to pre-school and school children by the Ministry of Health shall
be gratuitous.
(2) There is payable for all other dental services provided by the Ministry of Health
such fees and charges as may be prescribed from time to time by the Minister
in charge of the Ministry of Health.
(3) All fees paid pursuant to subsection (2) form part of the Cook Islands
Government Account.
[Section 7(1): amended, on 29 April 1974, by Schedule 2 of the Ministry of Social Services
Act 1973–74 (1973–74 No 35); on 27 December 1984, by Schedule Part 2 of the Ministry of Health
Act 1984 (1984 No 19).]
[Section 7(2): amended, on 29 April 1974, by Schedule 2 of the Ministry of Social Services

8 Liability of the Crown
(1) Where damage is suf fered by any person as a result of any wilful or negligent
act or omission of the Principal Dental Of f icer, or any dental of f icer or other
person duly authorised to practice any branch of dentistry , and who is a member
of the Public Service and is acting in the course of his or her employment, an
action in respect of the damage lies against the Crown by or on behalf of the
person suf fering the damage, and in any such case the Crown is liable in the
same manner and to the same extent as if the damage had been caused by an act
or omission of a servant of the Crown acting in the course of his or her
employment.
(2) Nothing in this section shall be deemed to af fect any right of action against the
person guilty of the act or omission complained of.
