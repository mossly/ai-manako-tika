An Ordinance to provide for the licencing of radio receiving sets and for the payment of
licence fees.
Part 1
Preliminary
1 Short Title
This Ordinance may be cited as the Cook Islands Radio Licence
Ordinance 1964.
2 Commencement
This Ordinance shall come into force on 1 April 1965.
3 Interpretation
In this Ordinance, unless the context otherwise requires—
holder means the person or ﬁrm named in a trading licence issued in terms of
the Regulations under the Cook Islands Act 1915 to provide for the issue of
trading licenses
premises means any building site, vehicle, or structure, or any group of
buildings within the same site or boundary and forming part of the same
establishment, or any part of any building or site under separate accommodation
or tenancy; and any room, suite of rooms, ﬂat, or apartment rented or engaged
by guests in any hotel or other accommodation house shall be regarded as
separate premises
radio receiving set means any apparatus intended for the purpose of af fecting
radio communication by reception of signs, signals, writing, images, and sounds
or intelligence of any nature by means of electromagnetic waves between
10 kc/s and 3,000,000 mc/s over paths which are not provided by any material
substance constructed or arranged for that purpose
registered co-operative society means a co-operative society registered under
the Cook Islands Co-operative Regulations 1953
trading licence means any licence, issued under the Regulations under the
Cook Islands Act 1915 to provide for the issue of trading licences, which is in
force and which entitles the person or ﬁrm named thereon to sell radio receiving
sets in the Cook Islands.

Part 2
Imposition and collection of licence fees
4 Licence Fees
(1) Subject to the provisions of this Ordinance every person who either operates or
permits the operation of a radio receiving set in the Cook Islands on or after
1 April 1965 shall pay a licence fee of 1 pound provided—
(a) that the payment of the licence fee in respect of any radio receiving set
shall relieve any other person from the liability of making a further
payment in respect of the same radio receiving set:
(b) that ownership shall determine the liability of any person to pay a licence
fee except that if ownership is disputed or if the reputed owner is absent
from the Cook Islands then the head of the household or the user or the
manager of the premises in which the radio receiving set is kept shall be
liable for the payment of the licence fee:
(c) that any person who has paid a licence fee in terms of the Radio
Regulations 1953 on a radio receiving set for each of the years ended
31 March 1962, 1963, 1964 and 1965 shall be entitled to receive a licence
on that receiving set without further payment.
(2) A licence fee shall not be required to be paid on any radio receiving set which
is held by the holder of a trading licence or by a registered co-operative society
for the purpose of sale.
(3) Subject to the preceding subsection, a licence fee of 1 pound shall be paid on
every radio receiving set irrespective of the time that it is used or remains in the
Cook Islands.
5 Payment of Licence Fees
(1) The licence fee in respect of every radio receiving set present on the island of
Rarotonga shall be paid to the Treasurer of the Cook Islands or if the radio
receiving set is present outside of Rarotonga it shall be paid to the Resident
Agent or Clerk in Charge of the island concerned.
(2) Every holder of a trading licence and every registered co-operative society shall
pay a licence fee of 1 pound on every radio receiving set sold on and after
1 April 1965 and shall, in addition, complete the prescribed form by inserting
the full name and address, of the purchaser, a full description of the radio
receiving set and the amount of the licence fee.
(3) The original of the prescribed form referred to in the preceding subsection shall
be handed to the purchaser of the radio receiving set and the duplicate shall be
forwarded together with the licence fee to the Treasurer of the Cook Islands or
Resident Agent or Clerk in Charge.
(4) Every person who is not the holder of a trading licence or who is not a registered
co-operative society and who imports a radio receiving set into the Cook Islands
on or after 1 April 1965 shall pay a licence fee of 1 pound.
6 Licences
(1) Every person who pays the licence fee of 1 pound in terms of this Ordinance
shall receive an acknowledgement of such payment on the prescribed form.

(2) Every person who purchases or obtains possession of a radio receiving set from
any person other than the holder of a trading licence or a registered co-operative
society shall notify the Treasurer of the Cook Islands or the Resident Agent or
Clerk in Charge as is appropriate and shall furnish such particulars as are
necessary to enable the transfer to be recorded.
(3) Every licence shall be transferable in accordance with the preceding subsection.
(4) The prescribed form shall be numbered consecutively and shall be in the form

Part 3
Of fences and penalties
7 General Penalty
Every person who commits an of fence against this Ordinance for which no other
penalty is provided shall be liable to a ﬁne not exceeding 10 pounds.
8 Defrauding the Revenue
Every person who commits any of fence against this Ordinance or does any other
act, with intent in either case to defraud the revenue by evading or enabling any
other person to evade payment of the licence fee on any radio receiving set shall
be liable to a ﬁne not exceeding 50 pounds.

9 Revocation

Islands.
