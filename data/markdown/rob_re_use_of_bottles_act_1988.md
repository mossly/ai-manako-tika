An Act to provide for the re-use of bottles
BE IT ENACTED by the Parliament of the Cook Islands in Session assembled and by
the authority of the same as follows:
1 Short title
This Act may be cited as the Re-use of Bottles Act 1988.
2 Interpretation
In this Act,—
bottle means any carboy ﬂagon bottle jar pot tubular and other similar container
of glass plastic or ceramic manufacture of any kind commonly used for the
conveyancing storage packing or sale of liquids whether such liquids are
intended for human consumption or otherwise.
3 Deemed ownership of bottles
(1) Despite any rule of law or equity to the contrary , any person who lawfully
comes into possession of an empty bottle or comes into possession of a bottle by
virtue of having purchased its contents, becomes the lawful owner of all rights
and interests both in law and equity in and pertaining to that bottle and all prior
rights and interests of whatsoever nature in respect of that bottle and vested in
any other person shall cease and determine.
(2) The provisions of this section have ef fect despite anything to the contrary
stamped embossed printed or otherwise indicated on the bottle or its labelling or
packaging.
4 Re-use of bottles
(1) Where any person becomes the owner of a bottle pursuant to section 3, then
neither that person nor any subsequent owner is answerable to any former owner
of that bottle as to its subsequent use re-use or disposal except in circumstances
where any re-use of that bottle amounts to the passing of f of its contents as being
the product of a prior owner.
(2) The re-use of a bottle by reﬁlling that bottle with a liquid of the same or
substantially similar nature as that which the bottle originally contained, does
not of itself amount to a passing of f even if the bottle may be of some peculiar
or distinctive shape provided that the person re-using that bottle has labelled the
bottle to identify fairly its contents and the origin of those contents.
This Act is administered by the Department of Trade, Labour and Transport.
Published under the authority of the Parliament of the Cook Islands.
