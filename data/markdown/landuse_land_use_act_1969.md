An Act to provide for designation of land use within the Cook Islands.
BE IT ENACTED by the Legislative Assembly of the Cook Islands in Session
assembled, in accordance with the provisions of subclause (1) of Article 41 of the
Constitution of the Cook Islands and by the authority of the same, as follows:
1 Short title and commencement
(1) This Act may be cited as the Land Use Act 1969.
(2) This Act comes into force on the island of Rarotonga on the day of assent.
(3) This Act comes into force with regard to any other island on the date to be
appointed by the Queen’s Representative by Order in Executive Council and
dif ferent dates may be so appointed in respect of dif ferent islands.
[Section 1(3): amended, on 6 April 1982, by section 4(3) of the Constitution Amendment (No 10)
Act 1981–82 (1981–82 No 20).]
2 Interpretation
In this Act unless the context, otherwise requires—
Board means the Land Use Board established by this Act
Minister means the Minister responsible for Justice
occupier means and includes owner, trustee, lessee or other user of land and any
person having an interest in land by native custom.
Part 1
Zoning orders
3 Zoning orders
The Queen’s Representative may by Order in Executive Council (in this Act
referred to as a zoning order) establish in any island of the Cook Islands a zone
or zones of land use.
[Section 3: amended, on 6 April 1982, by section 4(3) of the Constitution Amendment (No 10) Act
1981–82 (1981–82 No 20).]
4 Ef fect of zoning order
After the making of a zoning order the use of the land within the zone created
shall be pursuant to the provisions of the zoning order, and this Act and such
order shall not in any way be deemed an acquisition by the Crown of any right
or interest in the land af fected.
5 Nature of zoning orders
(1) Zoning orders may provide for the use of land in any 1 or more of the following
respects:—
(a) the use of land primarily for public recreation and enjoyment:
(b) the use of land primarily for tourist accommodation:
(c) the use of land primarily for residential purposes:
(d) the use of land primarily for industrial purposes:
(e) the use of land primarily for commercial purposes:
(f) the use of land primarily for agricultural purposes:
(g) the use of land primarily for public works including roads.

(2) Any zoning order may also include provision for any use incidental or
subsidiary to the primary use designated.
6 Continuing use by occupier
Upon a zoning order being made in respect of any land any occupier may
continue to use the same in the manner the land was being used at the date of
making of the zoning order but must not, except in accordance with the terms of
the zoning order, thereafter make any permanent improvement or alteration to
the land or erect or add to any building or other erection thereon without the
prior consent of the Board.
7 Ef fect of ceasing non-conforming use
If at any time while a zoning order is in force the occupier of any land af fected
thereby has been lawfully using the said land in a manner contrary to the
provisions of the zoning order and ceases so to use the said land for a period of
12 months such occupier is not entitled to resume the non-conforming use of the
said land without ﬁrst obtaining the consent of the Board. If the Board, after
hearing application by the occupier, refuses to grant permission to use the land
in the manner in which it was previously used then the occupier is entitled to
request the Crown to take over the said land and pay appropriate compensation
therefor in accordance with the provisions of section 357 of the Cook Islands
Act 1915.
8 Failure to comply with zoning order
Except where section 6 or an order of the Board applies, any person who fails
to comply with any zoning order is guilty of an of fence and is liable on
conviction to a ﬁne not exceeding $100 and in the case of a continuing of fence
to a further ﬁne not exceeding $20 for every day on which the of fence is
continued.

Part 2
Land Use Board
9 Land Use Board
(1) There is hereby established for the purposes of this Act a Board to be called the
Land Use Board.
(2) The Board shall consist of not more than 5 members, 1 of whom is the Chief
Judge of the Land Court who shall be Chairperson. The remaining members of
the Board must be appointed by the Minister and hold of f ice at his or her
pleasure but may resign. Any member may be re-appointed.
(3) No person is deemed to be employed in the service of Her Majesty for the
purposes of the Public Service Act 1965 by reason of his or her being a member
of the Board.
(4) The members of the Board must be paid for their services such remuneration
travelling expenses and allowances as the Minister responsible for ﬁnance from
time to time determines in that behalf.
10 Functions of Board
(1) The functions of the Board are to hear and determine—
(a) objections or submissions by any occupier or other person or group of
persons in respect of any zoning order or proposed zoning order:
(b) any application by an occupier of land for permission to deviate from a
zoning order:
(c) any application other than matters referred to in paragraphs (a) and (b) by
any person concerning the use of land arising from the provisions of this
Act or regulations thereunder or any zoning or other order pursuant to
this Act.
(2) The Board may also either at the request of the Minister or of its own motion
report and make recommendations to the Minister on the following matters:
(a) the need to establish any zone or class of zones and the conditions to be
attached thereto:
(b) the alteration or rescission of any zoning order.
11 Powers of Board
The Board has all the powers necessary to carry out its functions under this Act
and may conduct hearings in public or otherwise as it deems ﬁt.
12 Proceedings of Board
Proceedings of the Board are pursuant to regulations under this Act and in the
absence of any appropriate regulation the Board may regulate its own
procedure.
13 Matters to be considered by Board in determining any application or
matter
In considering any application or matter before it the Board must have regard
to—

(a) the interests of the public generally which is the paramount
consideration:
(b) the needs of an island or part of an island in relation to the use of the land
concerning which the application or matter arises:
(c) any hardship imposed on any occupier or other person af fected by the
determination of the Board.
14 Lapsing of permission to deviate from a zoning order
All decisions of the Board granting permission to deviate from the terms of a
zoning order lapse at the expiry of 10 years from the date of the granting of
permission or on the rescission or alteration of the zoning order whichever event
happens ﬁrst.
15 Liability of Board members
No member of the Board is personally liable for any act or thing done by the
Board or while acting pursuant to a decision of the Board.

Part 3
Administration
16 Administration
Subject to the control of the Minister this Act must be administered by the
Department of Justice which is responsible for the enforcement of all zoning
and other orders made pursuant to this Act.
17 Regulations
(1) The Queen’s Representative may from time to time by Order in Executive
Council, in accordance with recommendations thereon made to him or her by
the Minister, make all such regulations as may , in his or her opinion, be deemed
necessary or expedient for giving full ef fect to the provisions of this Act and for
the due administration thereof.
(2) Without limiting the general power hereinbefore conferred it is hereby declared
that regulations may be made in this connection for all or any of the following
purposes—
(a) prescribing the procedure to be followed in respect of the applications
and objections to and proceedings before the Board and prescribing fees
to be paid in respect of any such applications or proceedings:
(b) providing for the keeping of records for the purposes of this Act:
(c) prescribing forms for applications and other documents required under
this Act:
(d) providing the procedure for the issue, advertisement and service of
notices, and documents under this Act:
(e) prescribing ﬁnes not exceeding $100 in any case for failure to comply
with the provision of any regulation under this Act:
(f) providing for the appointment of such staf f and accommodation as is
required to enable the Board to carry out its functions under this Act.
(3) All regulations made under this section must be laid before Parliament within
28 days after the date of their making if Parliament is then in session, and, if not,
must be laid before Parliament within 28 days after the date of the
commencement of the next ensuing session.
[Section 17(1): amended, on 6 April 1982, by section 4(3) of the Constitution Amendment (No 10)
Act 1981–82 (1981–82 No 20).]
This Act is administered by the Justice Department.
