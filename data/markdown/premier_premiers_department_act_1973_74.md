An Act to constitute the Premier’s Department.
BE IT ENACTED by the Legislative Assembly of the Cook Islands in Session
assembled, and by the authority of the same, as follows:
1 Short title
This Act may be cited as the Premier’s Department Act 1973–74.
2 Interpretation
In this Act, unless the context otherwise requires—
Chief Administration Of ficermeans a Chief Administration Of f icer appointed
under this Act
Department means the Premier’s Department constituted under this Act
Outer Island means any Island in the Cook Islands except the island of
Rarotonga
Outer Islands means all the islands in the Cook Islands except the island of
Rarotonga
Premier means the Prime Minister of the Cook Islands appointed under the
provision of the Constitution
Secretary means the Secretary of the Premier’s Department appointed under the
provisions of this Act
Senior Clerk means a Senior Clerk appointed under the provisions of this Act.
Part 1
Premier’s Department
3 Premier’s Department
There shall be a Department of State to be known as the Premier’s Department
which under the control of the Prime Minister is charged with the administration
of this Act and with such other functions as may be lawfully conferred on it.
4 Divisions of Department
(1) There shall be 2 Divisions of the Department responsible to the Prime Minister
for such functions as may be prescribed by this Act or any other enactment and
for such other functions as the Prime Minister may deem necessary .
(2) The 2 Divisions of the Department are—
(a) Central Administration Division:
(b) Outer Islands Af fairs Division.

Part 2
Central Administration Division
5 Functions of Central Administration Division
The functions of the Central Administration Division are—
(a) to co-ordinate the administration of Government on behalf of Cabinet:
(b) to provide such clerical, secretarial and other services as may be
necessary to facilitate the operations of Cabinet and the Executive
Council:
(c) to provide a channel of communication between the Government of the
Cook Islands and the Government of New Zealand through the of f ice of
the Queen’s Representative of the Cook Islands:
(d) to provide a channel of communication between the Government of the
Cook Islands and other sectors of the community:
(e) to establish and control a records system for use by the Prime Minister
and Cabinet:
(f) to operate and manage a pool of typists for use by Departments of State
and other agencies of Government:
(g) to carry out such other functions as the Prime Minister may from time to
time deem necessary .
[Section 5(c): amended, on 6 April 1982, by section 4(3) of the Constitution Amendment (No 10)
Act 1981–82 (1981–82 No 20).]
6 Secretary of Premier’s Department
(1) There must from time to time be appointed as a member of the Public Service
a Secretary of the Premier’s Department who is the administrative head of the
Central Administration Division of the Department.
(2) The person holding of f ice at the commencement of this Act as the Secretary of
the Premier’s Department is deemed to have been appointed to that of f ice under
this Act.
7 Other of ficers and employees
(1) There may from time to time be appointed as members of the Public Service
such other of f icers and employees of the Central Administration Division of the
Premier’s Department as may be necessary .
(2) All of f icers and employees must act under the direction of the Secretary of the
Premier’s Department in the exercise and performance of the powers, duties and
functions conferred or imposed on them by an enactment of which the Central
Administration Division or the Secretary of the Premier’s Department is
charged with the administration by this Act or any other enactment.
(3) All persons (except those elsewhere in this Act excluded) who at the
commencement of this Act are of f icers and employees of the Department are
deemed to have been appointed to the Central Administration Division under
this Act.

Part 4
Outer Islands Af fairs Division
11 Functions of Outer Islands Af fairs Division
The functions of the Outer Islands Af fairs Division are—
(a) to establish and control an administrative centre on each Outer Island:
(b) to provide a channel of communication between the Prime Minister and
the Outer Islands:
(c) to administer the provisions of any legislation enacted with speciﬁc
provision to the outer islands:
(d) to coordinate the operations of each Ministry and the local government
on each Outer Island:
(e) to carry out such other functions as may be prescribed by any other
enactment or as the Prime Minister may deem necessary .
12 Director of Outer Islands Af fairs
There must from time to time be appointed as a member of the Public Service
a Director of Outer Island Af fairs who is stationed in Rarotonga and is the
administrative head of the Outer Islands Af fairs Division of the Department.
13 Chief Administration Of ficers
There may from time to time be appointed as members of the Public Service
Chief Administration Of f icers for the Outer Islands who subject to the control of
the Director is responsible for the administration of the functions prescribed by
section 14.
14 Functions of Chief Administration Of ficers
The Chief Administration Of f icers have the following principal functions—
(a) to act as principal representatives of the Government of the Cook Islands:
(b) to provide a channel of communication between the local governments
and the central government in Rarotonga:
(c) to encourage within local custom and land usage the economic and social
development of the people.
15 Senior Clerk
There must from time to time be appointed as a member of the Public Service
a Senior Clerk for each outer island who subject to the control of the Chief
Administration Of f icer, if any , is responsible for the administration of the
functions prescribed by section 16.
16 Functions of Senior Clerk
The Senior Clerk has the following functions—
(a) to aid and assist the Chief Administration Of f icer in encouraging
economic and social development and in the absence of the Chief
Administration Of f icer to act in his or her place:
(b) to control the collection and expenditure of public moneys, the control of
all Government stores and the responsibility for all Government
buildings:

administration of Government:
(d) to control all staf f and employees appointed to carry out these functions:
(e) to carry out such other functions as may be required of him or her by the
Chief Administration Of f icer.

18 Principal functions of Co-ordinating Committee
The principal functions of the Co-ordinating Committee are—
(i) to co-ordinate all services necessary for the ef fective economic and social
development of their island:
(ii) to be responsible for the control and discipline of all public servants on
their island within the provisions of any delegation made by the Public
Service Commissioner pursuant to the Public Service Act 1969:
(iii) to carry out such other functions relevant to their Island that may be
prescribed by any enactment or deemed necessary by the Prime Minister.
19 Other of ficers and employees
(1) There may from time to time be appointed as members of the Public Service
such other of f icers and employees of the Outer Islands Af fairs Division of the
Premier’s Department as may be necessary both in Rarotonga and in the Outer
Islands.
(2) All of f icers and employees must act under the direction of the Director of Outer
Island Af fairs in the exercise and performance of the powers, duties and
functions conferred or imposed on them by any enactment of which the Outer
Islands Af fairs Division or the Director is charged with the administration by
this Act or any other enactment.
(3) All persons in the outer islands (other than Resident Agents and
Clerks-In-Charge) who at the commencement of this Act are of f icers and
employees of the Premier’s Department are deemed to have been appointed to
the Outer Islands Af fairs Division of the Premier’s Department under this Act.

Other provisions
20 Research Of ficer
There shall from time to time be appointed as a member of the Public Service
a Research Of f icer within the Department who are directly responsible to the
Prime Minister for undertaking such research as the Prime Minister may deem
necessary .
21 Personal Secretaries to Prime Minister
(1) There shall from time to time be appointed as members of the Public Service not
more than 2 Personal Secretaries to the Prime Minister who are directly
responsible to the Prime Minister and who must perform such duties or
functions as the Prime Minister may direct.
(2) The person holding of f ice at the commencement of this Act as Personal
Secretary to the Prime Minister is deemed to have been appointed to that of f ice
under this Act.
22 Special investigations
(1) The Prime Minister may from time to time, as he or she thinks ﬁt, cause an
investigation pursuant to this section to be made into any matter connected with
or af fecting any of the functions of the Department.
(2) For the purpose of any such investigation, the Prime Minster may , by order in
writing signed by him, require any person, partnership, company or other
corporate body carrying on any business relating to or af fecting the subject
matter of the investigation to produce for the inspection of any person
nominated in that behalf by the Prime Minister, any papers, books or documents
which are in possession of the person, partnership, company or corporate body ,
or to set down in writing any facts relating to the subject matter of the
investigation which are within the knowledge of the person, partnership,
company , or corporate body , and to allow copies or extracts from any such
papers, books or documents to be made by the person inspecting them.
(3) Any person who fails to comply with any order of the Prime Minister under this
section or who deceives or obstructs or attempts to deceive or obstruct any
person nominated by the Prime Minister as aforesaid in the conduct of any such
investigation commits an of fence, and is liable on conviction to a ﬁne not
exceeding $100.
(4) All information obtained by the Prime Minister or by any other person by means
of any such investigation must be treated as conﬁdential except for the purpose
of this Act.
23 Annual reports
(1) The Secretary of the Premier’s Department and the Director of Outer Islands
Af fairs must as soon as practicable after the end of each ﬁnancial year furnish
to the Prime Minister an individual report on the operations of their respective
Divisions for that year.

been furnished to the Prime Minister if Parliament is then in session, and, if not,
must be laid before Parliament within 28 days after the commencement of the
next ensuing session.

24 Regulations
(1) The Queen’s Representative, by Order in Executive Council, may from time to
time make all such regulations as are deemed necessary or expedient for the
purpose of giving full ef fect to this Act and for the due administration thereof.
(2) All regulations made under this section must be laid before Parliament by the
Prime Minister within 28 days of the date of their making if Parliament is then
in session, and, if not, must be laid before Parliament within 28 days after the
date of commencement of the next ensuing session.
