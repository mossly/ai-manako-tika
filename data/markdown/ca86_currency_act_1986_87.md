4 Currency system established
The monetary unit, or unit of currency , of the Cook Islands and the
denominations of money in the currency are those applying in New Zealand.
[Section 4: replaced, on 8 June 1995, by section 2 of the Currency Amendment
Act 1994–95 (1994–95 No 31).]
5 Contracts, etc, to be made in New Zealand currency
Every sale, payment, bill of exchange, promissory note, and security for money ,
and every contract, agreement, deed instrument, transaction, dealing, matter, and
thing whatsoever relating to money , or involving the payment of or a liability to
pay any money that is made, executed, entered into, or done in the Cook Islands
is made executed, entered into, or done according to the currency of New
Zealand unless it is expressly made, executed, entered into or done according to
the currency of a country other than New Zealand.
[Section 5: replaced, on 8 June 1995, by section 3 of the Currency Amendment
Act 1994–95 (1994–95 No 31).]

Coinage
6 Standards for coins
The standard denominations, composition, weight and design of coins are as
from time to time determined by the Minister acting with the concurrence of
Cabinet and published by notice in the Gazette, provided that a remedy in
respect of weight (that is to say , a variation from the standard weight applicable
to the coin) may be allowed as determined.
[Section 6: amended, on 16 June 2005, by section 3 of the Currency Amendment Act 2005 (2005
No 3).]
7 Issuing or authorising coins
The Minister acting with the concurrence of Cabinet may , by notice in the
Gazette:
(a) cause to be issued any coin as legal tender; and
(b) authorise any coin which, by virtue of the laws for the time being of New
Zealand, is legal tender in New Zealand, as legal tender in the Cook
Islands.
[Section 7: amended, on 16 June 2005, by section 4 of the Currency Amendment Act 2005 (2005
No 3).]
8 Calling in coins
(1) The Minister may , by notice in the Gazette, call in any coins of any date or
denomination made and issued or authorised under this Act, or any coins
bearing dates earlier than a date speciﬁed in the notice, and on the taking ef fect
of the notice, all coins to which it applies cease to be legal tender in the Cook
Islands, but the Treasury Department continues to be liable to pay any such coin
on presentation at the of f ice of the Treasury Department, and is entitled to
recover from the Public Account the value of any coin so paid out of money
appropriated by Parliament for that purpose.
(2) Every notice under this section has ef fect from such date as is speciﬁed in the

9 Existing coins
The coins in the existing currency named and described in the regulations set
out in Schedule 1 are deemed for all purposes to be coins duly issued or
authorised pursuant to the provisions of this Act.
10 Prohibition of other than of ficial coins
(1) No person may make or issue, or cause to be made or issued, any piece of gold,
silver, copper, bronze, or nickel or of any metal or mixed metal, of any value
whatsoever, (other than a coin made and issued or authorised under this Act) as
a coin or as a token for money or as purporting that the holder thereof is entitled
to demand any monetary or other value denoted on it.

11 Legal tender
(1) A tender of payment of money , if it is made in coins made and issued or
authorised under this Act, and of current weight, is a legal tender:—
(a) in the case of coins of the denomination of $1, or more, for the payment
of any amount:
(b) in the case of coins of the denomination of 5 cents, 10 cents, 20 cents, or
50 cents, for the payment of an amount not exceeding $5:
(c) in the case of any other coins, for the payment of an amount not
exceeding 20 cents.
(2) For the purpose of this section, a coin is deemed to be not of current weight if
it has become diminished in weight by wear or otherwise so as to be of less
weight than the weight determined pursuant to section 6 as the least current
weight of that coin.
(3) The references to coins in subsection (1) do not include references to coins that
have been called in pursuant to this Act.

12 New Zealand bank notes
Every bank note which is legal tender in New Zealand is legal tender in the
Cook Islands to the amount expressed in the note.
[Section 12: replaced, on 8 June 1995, by section 4 of the Currency Amendment

13 Issue of bank notes by Minister

notes of the denomination of $3 as legal tender in the Cook Islands for that
amount.
(2) The Minister may determine the form design, content and material of bank notes
issued by it.

General provisions
16 Cost of coins and bank notes
The cost of all coins and bank notes made and issued under the provisions of
this Act including the cost of design, manufacture, freight, insurance, and all
incidental expenses, must be paid out of the Public Account from money
appropriated by Parliament for that purpose.
17 Advisory and technical committees
(1) The Minister may appoint advisory or technical committees for the purpose of:
(a) making recommendations with respect to coins, bank notes and currency:
(b) carrying out investigations and surveys for the purpose of obtaining
information for any of the purposes of this Act:
(c) performing such other functions in relation to the provisions of this Act
as may lawfully be directed by the Minister.
(2) The members of such advisory or technical committees must be paid such
remuneration, expenses and allowances as may be determined by the Minister.

pursuant to the guarantee provided for in subsection (1).
