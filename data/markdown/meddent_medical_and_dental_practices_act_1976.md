and regulate private practice.

Part 1
Medical and Dental Council
3 Medical and Dental Council established
(1) There is hereby established a Council to be known as the Medical and Dental
Council of the Cook Islands.
The Council is a body corporate with perpetual succession and a common seal
and may hold property and sue and be sued and do and suf fer all acts and things
bodies corporate may do and suf fer.

(2) The Council is responsible to the Minister in charge of health.
4 Membership of Council
(1) The Council consists of—
(a) the Secretary for Health:
(b) 3 persons appointed annually by the Minister on the advice of the Cook
Islands Medical and Dental Association Incorporated who are registered
Medical and Dental Practitioners and of whom at least 1 is a registered
Dental practitioner:
(c) 1 person who is a member of the general public appointed annually by
the Minister.
(2) The Council at its ﬁrst meeting and each year thereafter must appoint from its
members who are medical or dental practitioners a Chairperson and a Deputy
Chairperson.
(3) The Chairperson must preside at all meetings of the Council at which he or she
is present. If at any meeting of the Council the Chairperson for the time being
is not present the Deputy Chairperson must preside at the meeting.
(4) The Council must appoint a secretary to the Medical and Dental Council of the
Cook Islands and such other of f icers, servants and agents as it thinks ﬁt, and
may pay them such remuneration as it considers appropriate.
(5) In the event of any person nominated as a member pursuant to subsection (1)(b)
ceasing to reside in Rarotonga such person is deemed to have completed his or
her term of of f ice.
5 Meetings of Council
(1) Meetings of the Council shall be held at such times and places as the
Chairperson may from time to time appoint but no less frequently than every
6 months.
(2) Every question before the Council must be determined by a majority of votes of
the members present at the meeting of the Council and voting thereon.
(3) The Chairperson has a deliberative vote and, in the case of equality of votes,
also has a casting vote.
(4) Except as expressly provided in this Act or in regulations made under this Act,
the Council may regulate its procedures in such manner as it thinks ﬁt.
6 Functions of Council
(1) The functions of the Council are to control professional and ethical standards of
medical and dental practice. Particularly—
(a) [Repealed]
(b) to regulate private medical and dental practices:
(c) to maintain discipline within the medical and dental professions:
(d) to advise and make recommendations to the Minister in respect of any
matter af fecting medical or dental practices in the Cook Islands.
(2) The Council must make such decisions, determinations, give such directions and
consents and do such other acts and things as are provided for in this Act and as
may be necessary for the ef fective administration of this Act.

Part 2
Registration
9 Qualiﬁcation for conditional registration
Subject to section 18 every person is entitled to be conditionally registered as a
medical or dental practitioner under this Act who satisﬁes the Council that he or
she is—
(a) the holder of a medical or dental degree, diploma or equivalent
qualiﬁcation from—
(i) Australia, Fiji, New Zealand, the Republic of Ireland or the
United Kingdom; or
(ii) any other country provided that the Council is satisﬁed that the
standard of such qualiﬁcation is comparable to that of the other
countries listed in subparagraph (i); and
(b) of good fame and character.
Provided that any person who is not a Cook Islander is not entitled as of right
to registration in the Cook Islands and the Council has the power to refuse
registration to such person if it is of the opinion that adequate numbers of
practitioners are available in the Cook Islands.
10 Ef fect of conditional registration
Any person conditionally registered under this Act is deemed for all purposes to
be registered as a medical or dental practitioner while they are practising
medicine or dentistry as the case may be as an of f icer of the Department of
Health but not otherwise.
11 Cancellation of conditional registration
(1) The Council may cancel the conditional registration of any person where it
considers that they are not a ﬁt person to be conditionally registered by reason
of the fact that—
(a) since the person’s registration they have been convicted of any of fence
punishable by imprisonment for a term of 2 years or more:
(b) the person is not otherwise of good fame or character:
(c) the person lacks professional competence.
(2) The Council must cancel the conditional registration of any person upon the
registration of that person as a medical or dental practitioner as hereinafter
provided.
12 Qualiﬁcation for registration as medical or dental practitioner
(1) Subject to section 18, every person is entitled to be registered as a medical or
dental practitioner under this Act who satisﬁes the Council that, for not less than
2 years—
(a) they have been conditionally registered as a medical or dental practitioner
under this Act; and
(b) they have practised as a medical or dental practitioner as an of f icer of the
Department of Health to the satisfaction of the Council.

12A Qualiﬁcation for registration as specialist
(1) Every person who is registered under this Act as a medical or dental practitioner
is entitled to be registered as a specialist on satisfying the Council that they—
(a) hold postgraduate qualiﬁcations in medicine or dentistry in the speciality
from—
(i) Australia, Fiji, New Zealand, the Republic of Ireland or the
United Kingdom; or
(ii) any other country provided the Council is satisﬁed that the
standard of such qualiﬁcation is comparable to that of the other
countries speciﬁed in subparagraph (i).
(2) Every person entitled to be registered as a specialist must be so registered by the
Council on application by him or her for such registration and on payment of the
fee prescribed in the Schedule.

13 T emporary registration
Where any person with medical or dental qualiﬁcation is visiting the Cook
Islands with the concurrence of or at the request of the Department of Health the
Council may on being satisﬁed with such qualiﬁcations issue him or her with a
certiﬁcate of temporary registration this period to be not more than 6 months
and to be stated on the certiﬁcate. Any certiﬁcate so issued must specify the
professional function to be undertaken during the visit.
For the purpose of this section, the Council must be satisﬁed regarding
qualiﬁcations if the person is—
(a) a member of the staf f of any recognised public hospital in New Zealand,
any medical school, any national health institution or any international
organisation:
(b) an intern or resident medical of f icer of any teaching hospital.
14 Ef fect of temporary registration
During the period speciﬁed in any certiﬁcate or any extension thereof issued
under section 13 the holder of the certiﬁcate is deemed for all purposes to be
registered as a medical or dental practitioner while he or she is practising
medicine or dentistry in the Department of Health in the function speciﬁed in
the certiﬁcate but not otherwise.

18 Restrictions on registration
No person is entitled as of right to be conditionally registered, temporarily
registered, or registered as a medical or dental practitioner under this Act if he
or she is not a ﬁt person to be so registered by reason of the fact that—
(a) he has been at any time convicted of any of fence punishable by
imprisonment for a term of 2 years or more; or
(b) he is otherwise not of good fame or character.
19 Mode of registration
(1) Conditional registration and registration as a medical or dental practitioner must
be ef fected by the entry in the registers kept for the purpose by the Registrar of
the following particulars—
(a) the nature of registration:
(b) the name of the person registered:
(c) particulars of the qualiﬁcation by virtue whereof he or she is registered:
(d) the person’s address:
(e) the date of registration:
(f) such other particulars as may be prescribed.
(2) The Registrar must, on application in that behalf and on payment of the
prescribed fee, issue to that person a certiﬁcate of registration.

Part 3
Private practice
26 Private medical and dental practices to be licensed
(1) Any registered medical or dental practitioner intending to establish a private
medical or dental practice must apply to the Council for a licence to operate that
practice. Such application must include a statement of the type of practice to be
undertaken details of the location of premises, nature of equipment provided,
the method of maintaining patient records and any other matter it may require
from time to time to satisfy itself that the interests of patients are safeguarded.
(2) On being satisﬁed in these matters, the Council must approve the application
with any conditions necessary and on payment of the fee prescribed in Part 2 of
the Schedule by the practitioner the Council must issue a licence accordingly .
(3) Should the Council fail to be satisﬁed in these matters, the Council may reject
the application and so inform the applicant together with the reasons for the
rejection.
(4) The licence to undertake private medical or dental practice is valid for 1 year
from the date of issue. The Council must renew the licence each year on
application and payment of the fee prescribed in Part 2 of the Schedule by the
practitioner provided the Council is satisﬁed that any conditions of the licence
have been met and may when renewing such a licence add to or vary the
conditions of such licence.
(5) Any person who undertakes provides or carries out private medical or dental
practice without a licence from the Council pursuant to this section or not in
accordance with the conditions of such a licence commits an of fence and is
liable on conviction to a ﬁne not exceeding $5,000 and $100 for every day the
of fence continues.
(6) The requirements of this section are in addition to any requirement to hold a
practising certiﬁcate under any law .

Part 4
Discipline
27 Ethical standards to be observed
Registered medical and dental practitioners must not abuse the privileges
granted to them by the public and they must conduct themselves in accordance
with currently accepted ethical standards of professional behaviour.
28 Disciplinary powers
Persons registered under this Act are subject to the disciplinary procedures and
powers provided for in regulations made under the Ministry of Health Act.
[Section 28: replaced, on 13 June 2013, by section 38(2)(h) of the Ministry of Health Act 2013
(2013 No 6).]
29 T ransitional provision
(1) Despite any other provision of this Act any medical or dental practitioner in
active practice in the Cook Islands or employed in the Department of Health at
the commencement of this Act is entitled to be registered as a medical or dental
practitioner as the case may be provided he or she makes application for the
same within 3 months from the commencement of this Act.
(2) Despite any other provision of this Act any person in active private medical or
dental practice at the commencement of this Act is entitled to be licensed to
undertake private medical or dental practice as the case may be provided he or
she makes application for the same within 3 months from the commencement of
this Act.
(3) Despite any other provision of this Act until such time as the Council is
established in accordance with section 4 the function of the Council must be
performed by the Secretary for Health.
30 Regulations
(1) The Queen’s Representative may from time to time, by Order in Executive
Council, make all such regulations as may , in his or her opinion, be deemed
necessary or expedient from giving full ef fect to the provisions of this Act and
for the due administration hereof.
(2) All regulations made under this section must be laid before Parliament within
20 days after the date of the making thereof if Parliament is then in session, and,
if not, must be laid before Parliament within 28 days after the date of the
commencement of the next ensuing session.
[Section 30(1): amended, on 6 April 1982, by section 4(3) of the Constitution Amendment (No 10)
Act 1981–82 (1981–82 No 20).]
