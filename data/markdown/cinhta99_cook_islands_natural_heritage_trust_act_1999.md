An Act to establish a Cook Islands Natural Heritage Trust with the necessary resources
and powers to investigate, identify , research, study , classify , record, issue, preserve and
arrange publications, exhibitions, displays and generally educate the public on the science
of, and traditional practices and knowledge relating to, the ﬂora and fauna of the Cook
Islands.
BE IT ENACTED by the Parliament of the Cook Islands in Session assembled, and by
the authority of the same as follows:
1 Short title and commencement
(1) This Act may be cited as the Cook Islands Natural Heritage Trust Act 1999.
(2) This Act shall come into force on the date it is assented to by the Queen’s
Representative.

4 Cook Islands Natural Heritage T rust
(1) There is hereby established a Trust called the Cook Islands Natural Heritage
Trust.
(2) Except where inconsistent with the provisions of this Act, the laws of equity
relating to trusts apply to the Trust.
(3) The Trustee Act 1956 and the Public Service Act 1995–96 have no application
to the Trust.
5 Objects of T rust
(1) The objects of the Trust are to—
(a) investigate, identify , research, analyse, study , classify , record, integrate
and preserve scientiﬁc information and traditional knowledge of and
practices relating to ﬂora and fauna:
(b) make such information as is obtained pursuant to paragraph (a) generally
available to the public:
(c) assist Government departments and agencies upon request with the
supply of information obtained pursuant to paragraph (a).
(2) The objects of the Trust are deemed a Charitable purpose as that term is deﬁned
in section 2 of the Income T ax Act 1997.
6 Powers of T rust
(1) In the exercise of its functions under this Act, the Trust is empowered to—
(a) investigate, identify , research, analyse, study , classify , record, integrate
and preserve scientiﬁc information and traditional knowledge of and
practices relating to ﬂora and fauna:
(b) issue and arrange publications, exhibitions and displays, and generally
educate the public about ﬂora and fauna:
(c) make information in its possession available, with or without fee, to
Government departments and agencies:
(d) take such measures as it considers necessary to ensure that adequate
ﬁnance is available to carry out its objects including negotiating with
overseas agencies for such ﬁnance:
(e) erect suitable signs and notices at places of habitat or otherwise of
interest in respect of ﬂora or fauna:

(f) acquire by purchase, lease, exchange, bailment, gift, or otherwise any

(g) dispose of any of its property as it thinks ﬁt by sale, lease, exchange, or

(h) establish museums or other places displaying ﬂora and fauna accessible

(2) Wherever this Act or any other enactment provides for the Trust to carry out any

object shall be carried out and that power vests in the Board.
7 The Board
(1) The Board comprises the following Trustees—
(a) 1 person to be appointed by the Minister to be the Chairperson of the
Board:
(b) the Director of the Tu’anga T aporoporo:
(c) 1 person to be appointed by the Financial Secretary:
(d) 1 person to be appointed by the Secretary of Education:
(e) 1 person to be appointed by the Secretary of Agriculture with the
concurrence of the Secretary of Marine Resources:
(f) 1 person to be appointed by any settlor on the Trust whose settlement is
in monetary value equal to or greater than 50% of the amount of public
money settled on the Trust in the ﬁnancial year of the settlement or where
no public money has been settled on the Trust in that ﬁnancial year,
greater than 50% of the amount of public money settled in the last
ﬁnancial year in which public money was settled upon the Trust.
(2) The persons appointed Trustees pursuant to subsection (1) shall be appointed for
a term not exceeding 3 years provided however such appointees may from time
to time be reappointed.
(3) Any person appointed a Trustee by a settlor on the Trust in accordance with
subsection (1), remains a Trustee for—
(a) 1 year from the date of that person’s appointment; or
(b) for such time that the property or any part of the property settled upon the
Trust, remains unused or unexpended—
whichever is the greater.
(4) Subject to section 8, every Trustee continues in of f ice until his or her successor
comes into of f ice.

8 Extraordinary vacancies
(1) Any Trustee is deemed to have vacated of f ice if he or she is absent from 3
consecutive meetings of the Board without having ﬁrst obtained the leave of the
Board.
(2) If any Trustee—
(a) dies; or
(b) resigns; or
(c) is removed from of f ice; or
(d) is deemed to have vacated his or her of f ice—
the vacancy thereby occurring is deemed an extraordinary vacancy .
(3) Every extraordinary vacancy must be ﬁlled in the same manner as that of the
person whose of f ice has become vacant.
(4) Every person who ﬁlls an extraordinary vacancy holds of f ice for the remainder
of the term for which his or her predecessor was to hold of f ice.
(5) The powers of the Board is not af fected by any vacancy in its membership.
9 Meetings of Board
(1) Subject to subsection (5), the quorum for a meeting of the Board is 3 Trustees
including the Chairperson and despite any rule of law to the contrary , any
decision made by the Trustees at any meeting is valid and binding as if made by
all the Trustees.
(2) Meetings of the Board shall be held at such times and places as the Board
determines provided however the Board must meet not less than 3 times in any
ﬁnancial year and in any event whenever a period of 5 calendar months has
elapsed since its last meeting, the Board must meet forthwith.
(3) Except as is otherwise provided in this Act or by any other statute or rule of law ,
the Board may regulate its own procedure.
(4) The Chairperson shall preside at all meetings of the Board at which he or she is
present.
(5) In the absence of the Chairperson from any meeting, the Trustees present shall
appoint 1 of their number as Chairperson of that meeting.
(6) At least 7 days notice of any meeting of the Board shall be given to all Trustees.
(7) A resolution of the Trustees at any meeting held by the Board, is valid and
binding on all the Trustees.
10 Proceedings not af fected by certain irregularities
No act or proceeding of the Board or of any person acting as a Trustee shall be
invalidated by reason of a vacancy in the membership of the Board at the time
of the act or proceeding, or because of the subsequent discovery that there was
some defect in the appointment of any person so acting, or that such person was
incapable of being a Trustee or had ceased to become a Trustee.
11 T rustees not personally liable
(1) No Trustee is personally liable for any act or default by any Trustee or the Board
where that act was done or that default made in good faith and with reasonable
care and diligence provided however nothing herein contained af fects the
liability or obligations of the Board or any Trustee under any agreement or deed,
or pursuant to the Ministry of Finance and Economic Management
Act 1995–96.

12 Appointment of Director and other of ficers
(1) There shall be appointed by the Board, a Director to assist the Board in the
carrying out of the objects of the Trust.
(2) No person may be appointed as Director, other than a person considered by the

(3) The Director may , with the approval of the Board, appoint such other of f icers,
employees and consultants as may from time to time be required to carry out the
objects of the Trust.
(4) Every of f icer, employee and consultant engaged pursuant to this section is in the
performance of their duties responsible to the Director.

13 Funds of T rust
(1) The funds of the Trust consist of—
(a) all money received by the Trust out of money appropriated by Parliament
for the purposes of the Trust:
(b) all money lawfully contributed or donated or bequeathed to the Trust or
otherwise lawfully payable to the Trust:
(c) all money received by the Trust by way of fees, rent, charges for services
and publications, or otherwise in respect of any real or personal property
vested in or controlled by the Trust:
(d) all money received by the Trust from the sale or other disposal of any real
or personal property of the Trust:
(e) all accumulations of money belonging to the Trust.
(2) No monies may be withdrawn from any account of the Trust save pursuant to a
resolution passed by the Trustees at a meeting of the Board.
14 Accounts and audit
(1) Any funds of the Trust, as deﬁned in section 13, are deemed to be Public Money
as that term is deﬁned in the Ministry of Finance and Economic Management
Act 1995–96 despite its settlement upon the Trust and the Trust shall in any
ﬁnancial year it receives Public Money be deemed a Crown Agency .
(2) All funds settled upon the Trust must be paid into such Trust Account or
accounts as is authorised from time to time by the Financial Secretary .
15 Exempt importations
For the purposes of the V alue Added T ax Act 1997, despite any law to the
contrary , the Trust is by this Act deemed—
(a) an “approved aid project” pursuant to paragraph 4(a); and
(b) an “approved project” pursuant to paragraphs 4(b) and (c)—
of the Schedule 2 of the V alue Added T ax Act 1997.
16 Annual report
(1) The Trust must as soon as practicable after the end of each ﬁnancial year,
furnish to the Minister a report of the proceedings and operations of the Trust for
that ﬁnancial year, together with a copy of its accounts for that ﬁnancial year
certiﬁed by the Audit Of f ice.
(2) A copy of the report (including the accounts so certiﬁed attached thereto) must
be laid before Parliament as soon as practicable after it has been received by the
Minister.
17 Fees
There may be paid to the Trustees an annual allowance in such sum as may be
prescribed from time to time.
