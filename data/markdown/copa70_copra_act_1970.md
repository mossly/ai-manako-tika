An Act to provide for the inspection and grading of copra for export and the imposition
of an export duty on copra.
BE IT ENACTED by the Legislative Assembly of the Cook Islands in Session
assembled, and by the authority of the same, as follows:
1 Short title
This Act may be cited as the Copra Act 1970.

3 Inspection and weighing
(1) All copra for export must be inspected and weighed within 7 days before
shipment from the island in which it has been produced.
(2) The inspection must be carried out and the weighing must be supervised by an
Inspector.
4 Grading
(1) On inspection, copra for export must be graded as follows—
(a) copra which by appearance is white, free from bacterial stain, mould,
insect penetration, dust and burnt or black pieces, and which by touch is
dry and not soft or pliable, must be graded as First Grade Hot Air (Kiln)
dried:
(b) copra which by appearance is white with only slight bacterial stain or
pitting, free from mould, insect penetration, excessive dust and burnt or
black pieces, which by touch is dry and which contains not more than a
negligible proportion of soft pliable pieces, must be graded as First Grade
Hot Air (Sun) dried:
(c) copra which is dry and which may show some bacterial stain and pitting,
some superﬁcial mould, some superﬁcial insect penetration, some dust, a

negligible amount of slightly scorched pieces, no black pieces and which,

(2) Copra which is found on inspection to contain underdried, excessively mouldy

stones and any other foreign matter, or which has been contaminated by cement,
ﬂour, kerosene, diesel oil or any other foreign matter whatsoever must be
condemned prior to sale, barter or shipment as reject copra.
5 Prohibitions
No person may—
(a) make or attempt to make copra from other than mature coconuts; or
(b) pick any immature coconuts for the purpose of making copra therefrom;
or
(c) buy , sell, of fer for sale, store or ship any copra other than copra inspected
in accordance with this Act and graded as First Grade Hot Air (Kiln)
dried, First Grade Hot Air (Sun) dried, or Standard Grade; or
(d) of fer for export any reject copra.
6 Certiﬁcation
(1) No person may ship copra from any island without having ﬁrst obtained an
export certiﬁcate of weights and grades from an Inspector.
(2) The certiﬁcate must state the name of the consignor, the identiﬁcation marks, the
number of bags, the weights and grades and the amount of copra export duty
paid in accordance with section 8.
(3) All consignors of copra must be registered by the Secretary and must be
supplied with stencil mark plates.
[Section 6(3): amended, on 27 December 1978, by Schedule 2 of the Ministry of Agriculture and
Fisheries Act 1978 (1978 No 13).]
7 Packing, handling, and storage
(1) All copra for export must be packed in sound new standard type single weave
bags (or gunnies) to the nearest whole bag.
(2) The bags must be marked with aid of the stencil mark plate supplied by the
Secretary .
(3) No copra must be exported in ﬂour bags or sugar bags or any container other
than those described under subsection (1).
(4) Any copra, whether in bulk or bags, purchased or bartered, must be stored in
clean, well ventilated sheds with raised cement or wooden ﬂoor, and must be
well protected against insect infestation and rain damage.
[Section 7(2): amended, on 27 December 1978, by Schedule 2 of the Ministry of Agriculture and
Fisheries Act 1978 (1978 No 13).]

9 Registration and stencil fee
The fee for registration and each stencil mark plate is 50 cents.
10 Penalty for of fences
Any person who of fends against or fails to comply with any of the provisions of
this Act commits an of fence and is liable on conviction to a ﬁne not exceeding
$400 or to imprisonment for a term not exceeding 6 months.
11 Application of ﬁnes
All ﬁnes inﬂicted and money received under this Act must be paid into the Cook
Islands Government Account and form part of the public revenue of the Cook
Islands.
12 Repeal and revocation
The Copra Ordinance 1947 is hereby repealed and the Cook Islands Copra
Export Duty Regulations 1951 (SN 1951/291) are hereby revoked.
This Act is administered by the Agriculture Department.
Published under the authority of the Parliament of the Cook Islands.
