An Act to facilitate the 2009 Paciﬁc Mini Games and to protect the media and intellectual
property rights of the 2009 Paciﬁc Mini Games Limited and other sporting events and
organisations and for related purposes
BE IT ENACTED by the Parliament of the Cook Islands in Session assembled, and by
the authority of the same, as follows:
Part 1
Preliminary
1 Short title
This Act may be cited as the 2009 Paciﬁc Mini Games Act 2008.
2 Interpretation
In this Act unless the context otherwise requires—
2009 Paciﬁc Mini Games Limited is duly incorporated company of the Cook
Islands incorporated to facilitate the 2009 Paciﬁc Mini Games
Act means the 2009 Paciﬁc Mini Games Act 2008
Court means the High Court of the Cook Islands
CISNOC means the Cook Islands Sports and National Olympic Committee or
its successor, however styled, and in the event that CISNOC ceases to exist such
organisation or body representing sports organisations in the Cook Islands as
may be nominated by the Minister
event means any other event in the Cook Islands not being the games
games means the 2009 Paciﬁc Mini Games
intellectual property rights means the complete range of intangible property
rights, including but not limited to patents, trademarks, design rights and
copyright, in the names, titles, items, logos, matters, events and words, and any
other combination or derivative of words as listed in the Schedule to the Act
media rights means the right to report, broadcast, ﬁlm, photograph, record,
publish, televise, or distribute any report, photograph, ﬁlm, recording,
publication or broadcast of any event or activity of the 2009 Mini South Paciﬁc
Games in the Cook Islands through any publicly accessible media, including,
but not limited to, newspapers, radio, television, cinema, internet, books,
magazines, recordings and any computer based technology
Minister means the Minister of the 2009 Paciﬁc Mini Games
Secretary means the Head of the Ministry of Y outh and Sport.

Part 2
Protection of media rights
3 Media rights held by 2009 Paciﬁc Mini Games Limited
(1) Despite the provisions of any law , 2009 Paciﬁc Mini Games Limited holds the
media rights to the games.
(2) 2009 Paciﬁc Mini Games Limited may grant a licence or other right, in writing,
to a part or the whole of the media rights held by 2009 Paciﬁc Mini Games
Limited to any person on such terms as 2009 Paciﬁc Mini Games Limited, in its
absolute discretion, may determine.
4 Breach of media rights
(1) Any person who knowingly infringes the media rights of 2009 Paciﬁc Mini
Games Limited commits an of fence.
(2) Any person who knowingly aids or abets another person or counsels or procures
another person to commit an of fence under subsection (1) commits an of fence.
(3) Any person convicted of an of fence under subsections (1) or (2) is liable to a
ﬁne not exceeding $100,000 or imprisonment for 1 year or both a ﬁne and
imprisonment.
5 Seizure of unlawful media materials
(1) Despite the provisions of any law , a police of f icer, acting on a written request
from 2009 Paciﬁc Mini Games Limited, may—
(a) seize any document, photograph, recording, electronic record, ﬁlm or
other material from any person where such document, photograph,
recording, electronic record, ﬁlm or other material is believed on
reasonable grounds to have been created or brought into existence as part
of or as a consequence of a person committing an of fence under this Act;
or
(b) seize any camera, recording device, computer, microphone or any piece
of equipment from any person where such camera, recording device,
computer, microphone or piece of equipment is believed on reasonable
grounds to have been used or involved with the commission of an of fence
under this Act.
(2) Any item or object seized under this section must be held in safe keeping by the
Commissioner of Police Service and must be disposed of in accordance with
subsection (4).
(3) Any item or object seized under this section may be used in connection with a
prosecution for an of fence under this Act.
(4) Any item or object seized under this section which is found by the Court to have
been used or involved with the commission of an of fence under this Act or to
have been created or brought into existence as part of or as a consequence of a
person committing on of fence under this Act is forfeited to the Cook Islands and
in all other cases such item or object must be made available by the
Commissioner of Police Service for collection by the person from whom the
item or object was seized and in the event that no person claims such item or
object within 1 month after any court proceedings have been concluded or

Protection of intellectual property
6 Intellectual property rights held by 2009 Paciﬁc Mini Games Limited
(1) Despite the provisions of any other law , 2009 Paciﬁc Mini Games Limited holds
the intellectual property rights to the Games.
(2) 2009 Paciﬁc Mini Games Limited may grant a licence or other right, in writing,
to a part or the whole of the intellectual property rights held by 2009 Paciﬁc
Mini Games Limited to any person on such terms as 2009 Paciﬁc Mini Games
Limited, in its absolute discretion, may determine.
7 Breach of intellectual property rights
(1) Any person who knowingly infringes the intellectual property rights of 2009
Paciﬁc Mini Games Limited commits an of fence.
(2) Any person who knowingly aids or abets or counsels or procures another person
to commit an of fence under subsection (1) commits an of fence.
(3) Any person convicted of an of fence under subsections (1) or (2) is liable to a
ﬁne not exceeding $100,000 or imprisonment for 1 year or both a ﬁne and
imprisonment.
8 Seizure of material produced in breach of 2009 Paciﬁc Mini Games Limited
intellectual property rights
(1) Despite the provisions of any law , a police of f icer, acting on a written request
from 2009 Paciﬁc Mini Games Limited, may seize any item or object or other
material where such item or object or other material is believed on reasonable
grounds to have been created or brought into existence as part of or as a
consequence of a person committing an of fence under this Act.
(2) Any item or object or material seized under this section must be held in safe
keeping by the Commissioner of Police Service and must be disposed of in
accordance with subsection (4).
(3) Any item or object or material seized under this section may be used in
connection with the prosecution for an of fence under this Act.
(4) Any item or object or material seized under this section which is found by the
Court to have been created or brought into existence as part of or as a
consequence of a person committing an of fence under this Act is forfeited to the
Cook Islands and in all other cases such item or object or material must be made
available by the Commissioner of Police for collection by the person from
whom the item or object or material was seized and in the event that no person
claims such item or object or material within one month after any court
proceedings have been concluded or within one month after determination by
the Attorney General that no charge will be laid or prosecuted under this Act,
whichever event applies, such item or object or material is forfeited to the Cook
Islands.

Part 4
Miscellaneous
10 Minister may amend Schedule
(1) The Minister, with the consent of Cabinet, may amend the Schedule from time
to time by notice in the Gazette.
(2) The Minister, with the consent of Cabinet, may amend the Schedule to include
and allow for the protection of the intellectual property rights and media rights
relating to any other event or organisation in the Cook Islands.
11 Power to delegate
(1) 2009 Paciﬁc Mini Games Limited may delegate to any person any function or
power of 2009 Paciﬁc Mini Games Limited, other than this power of delegation.
(2) A delegate may sub-delegate to any person any function or power delegated by
2009 Paciﬁc Mini Games Limited if the delegate is authorised in writing to do
so by 2009 Paciﬁc Mini Games Limited.
12 Disclosure and misuse of information
(1) A person must not disclose any information obtained in connection with the
administration or execution of this Act unless the disclosure is made—
(a) with the consent of the person from whom the information was obtained;
or
(b) in connection with the administration or execution of this Act; or
(c) for the purpose of any legal proceedings arising out of this Act or of any
report of any such proceedings; or
(d) in accordance with a requirement of the Of f icial Information Act 2008,
Ombudsman Act 1984 or the Ministry of Finance and Economic
Management Act 1995–96; or
(e) with lawful excuse.
(2) Any person who breaches subsection (1) commits an of fence and upon
conviction is liable to a penalty of a ﬁne not exceeding $10,000.
(3) A person must not use, either directly or indirectly , commercial information
acquired by the person in the administration or execution of this Act for the
purpose of gaining either directly or indirectly any ﬁnancial advantage for the
person, the person’s spouse or de facto partner, a relative of the person or any
other associate of the person.
(4) Any person who breaches subsection (3) commits an of fence and upon
conviction is liable to a penalty of a ﬁne not exceeding $100,000.
(5) For the purposes of subsection (3) commercial information is information that is
not generally known but if generally known might reasonably be expected to
af fect the market value of any land or to inﬂuence materially any contract with
or tender for a contract with 2009 Paciﬁc Mini Games Limited.
