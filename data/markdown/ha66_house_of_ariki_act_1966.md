An Act to provide for the establishment of a House of Ariki and for the rights, powers,
functions and duties of the house and the members thereof.
WHEREAS by the Constitution of the Cook Islands as set forth in the Second Schedule
to the Cook Islands Constitution Amendment Act 1965 provision is made for a House of
Ariki of the Cook Islands and whereas by the Constitution certain provisions relating to
the establishment of the House were left to be prescribed by law and whereas it is now
desired to complete the establishment of the House of Ariki and to enact the provisions
necessary for that purpose.
BE IT ENACTED THEREFORE by the Legislative Assembly of the Cook Islands in
session, assembled and by the authority of the same, as follows:
1 Short title and commencement
(1) This Act may be cited as the House of Ariki Act 1966.
(2) This Act shall be deemed to have come into force on 1 January 1966.
2 Interpretation
Ariki means a person who has been invested with the title, rank, or of f ice of
Ariki in accordance with ancient custom prevailing in each of the Cook Islands
or in a group of 2 or more of the Cook Islands and includes in the case of an
Ariki who is under 18 years of age such person appointed by the family of the
Ariki to be trustee for such Ariki while under 18 years of age provided where
there are more than one Ariki on any Island or group of Islands represented in
the House of Ariki the trustee must have been approved also by the remaining
Ariki and if there are more than 2 remaining Ariki by a majority of the
remaining Ariki
Constitution means the Constitution of the Cook Islands as contained in the
Second Schedule to the Cook Islands Constitution Amendment Act 1965
together with any amendments to such Constitution
Executive Council means the Executive Council of the Cook Islands pursuant
to the Constitution
High Court means the High Court of the Cook Islands pursuant to the
Constitution
House means the House of Ariki and House of Ariki means the House of Ariki
provided by the Constitution
imprisonment includes the detention of a person by lawful authority whether
called imprisonment or not
Land Court means the Land Court of the Cook Islands pursuant to the
Constitution
Member means a member of the House
Minister means a member of the Cabinet of the Cook Islands pursuant to the
Constitution
Parliament means the Parliament of the Cook Islands
Premier means the Prime Minister of the Cook Islands pursuant to the
Constitution.

3 Establishment of House of Ariki
Subject to the provisions of the Constitution the establishment, rights, powers,
functions and duties of the House of Ariki and of the members and of f icers
thereof are as set forth in this Act.
4 Composition of House of Ariki
(1) The House comprises the following members:—
(a) one Ariki representing the Island of Aitutaki and the Island of Manuae:
(b) one Ariki representing the Island of Atiu:
(c) one Ariki representing the Island of Mangaia:
(d) one Ariki representing the Island of Manihiki and one representing the
Island of Rakahanga provided that if at any time either of such islands is
represented in the House then the Ariki representing the other island in
the House represents both islands until an Ariki is appointed from and to
represent the island for the time being not represented.
(e) one Ariki representing the Island of Mauke:
(f) one Ariki representing the Island of Mitiaro:
(g) one Ariki representing the Island of Penrhyn:
(h) one Ariki representing the Island of Pukapuka and the Island of Nassau:
(i) 6 Ariki representing the Island of Rarotonga and the Island of
Palmerston.
(2) The members of the House must be appointed by the Queen’s Representative. In
each case the member appointed must be an Ariki of the island or group of
islands he or she is to represent.
(3) If there is for the time being only one Ariki for any of the island or group of
islands speciﬁed in paragraphs (a) to (h) of subsection (1) that Ariki must be
appointed to be a member of the House as representing that island or group, but
if there are 2 or more Ariki for any such island or group the Queen’s
Representative must appoint the Ariki for that island or group who is nominated
for appointment as provided in subsection (5).
(4) If there are for the time being not more than 6 Ariki for the group of islands
comprising Rarotonga and Palmerston those Ariki must be appointed to be
members of the House as representing that group; but if there are more than 6
the Queen’s Representative must appoint the Ariki for that group who are
nominated for appointment as provided in subsection (5).
(5) In any case where there are more Ariki on any island or group of islands than the
number required to represent that island or group in the House as provided in
subsection (1) the Ariki of that island or group must together nominate the Ariki
who is or who are to represent the island or group in the House subject to the
following provisions—
(a) the of f ice of Ariki representing the island or group must rotate so that at
the end of each term the Ariki for the time being occupying the of f ice
must retire and his or her place taken for the following term by one of the
remaining Ariki until each Ariki on the island or group has served a term
in the House when the ﬁrst Ariki is nominated again and so on:
(b) in the event of any Ariki becoming disqualiﬁed to be a member or being
unable or unwilling to accept nomination to the House at any time the
next Ariki entitled to be nominated is eligible and the right to be
nominated rotates accordingly:

8 Functions of House of Ariki
The House has the following functions—
(1) It must consider such matters relative to the welfare of the people of the Cook
Islands as may be submitted to it by Parliament for its consideration, and it must
express its opinion and make recommendations thereon to Parliament.
(2) It may of its own motion make recommendations to Parliament upon any
question af fecting the customs or traditions of the Cook Islands or any of them
or of the inhabitants thereof provided that before considering any such motion
the President of the House invites the Prime Minister or any Minister or person
the Prime Minister appoints to be present and take part in the proceedings as if
present pursuant to section 10(3).
(3) Any recommendation to Parliament by the House must be accompanied where
applicable by an explanatory note of the reasons for the recommendation and
when any recommendation is not a unanimous decision of the members of the
House there must also be added to the explanatory note a concise statement of
the reasons of the minority of members of the House opposing the
recommendation. If for any reason any explanatory note has not been supplied
the President of the House must supply one when so requested by the Prime
Minister.

concerns the imposition of taxes or the appropriation or expenditure of the funds
or any part thereof of the Government of the Cook Islands unless requested so
to do by Parliament.

10 Attendance of Prime Minister and other persons at House of Ariki

is under consideration by the House.
(3) The President of the House when in his or her opinion any matter before the
House makes it desirable, may invite any member of Parliament to attend
meetings of the House relating to that matter.
(4) A person attending proceedings of the House by virtue of the provisions of

which he or she was invited to attend, as the case may be as if he or she were
a member of the House:
Provided that he or she is not entitled to vote in the House or any of its
committees.

11 Procedure
(1) The House must meet at Rarotonga at least once in every period of 12 months.
(2) The House must meet in session at Rarotonga at such time or times as
Parliament sits in session provided that if Parliament holds more sessions than
one in any period of 12 months it is not necessary to summon the House on the
occasion of each session of Parliament so long as the House meets at least once
during the period of 12 months following the date of the previous meeting.
(3) The House must meet at such other times and at such places as it is summoned
by the Queen’s Representative as provided in subsection (4).
(4) All meetings of the House must be summoned by the Queen’s Representative
acting on the advice of the Prime Minister. The House may be prorogued at any
time by the Queen’s Representative acting on the advice of the Prime Minister
by notice published in the Gazette and if at any time the of f ice of Prime Minister
is vacant the Queen’s Representative may at his or her discretion prorogue the
House by notice as aforesaid.
(5) The House must be presided over by one of its members who must be elected
by the members of the House pursuant to the Standing Orders of the House.
(6) Such presiding member must, unless earlier terminated or vacated or the
member dies, be called and referred to as the President of the House of Ariki and
holds of f ice for a period of 4 years or until a new president is elected by the
members of the House, whichever event happens ﬁrst. In the event of the
temporary absence of the President at any time the House may appoint a vice
president pursuant to Standing Orders of the House to act in the place of
President until his or her return.
(7) No business may be transacted at any meeting or sitting of the House if the
number of members present is less than 8.
(8) Subject to the provisions of the Constitution and to the provisions of this Act the
House may from time to time make, amend and repeal Standing Orders for the
regulation and orderly conduct of its proceedings and the despatch of business
provided that until such time as the House makes or adopts Standing Orders the
Standing Orders in the Schedule are the Standing Orders of the House.
(9) Subject to the provisions of the Constitution and the provisions of this Act the
House may determine its own procedure.
(10) The House is not disqualiﬁed for the transaction of business by reason of any
vacancy among its members and any proceedings therein are valid
notwithstanding that some person who was not entitled to do so sat or voted in
the House or otherwise took part in the proceedings.

13 Appointment of committee to hear disputed questions as to Ariki or
members of House of Ariki
(1) Should there be at any time any question as to whether an Ariki is qualiﬁed to
be a member of the House or has become disqualiﬁed from being a member, the
question must be referred for decision to a committee consisting of the Chief
Judge of the Land Court and 4 assessors appointed by the Executive Council. An
Ariki may be an assessor but shall not be so appointed if a member of the House
in any case where the question is whether the appointment of a member is
terminated and his or her seat vacated pursuant to any of the provisions of
section 7.
(2) The Committee appointed under the preceding subsection must proceed in such
manner as seems just and convenient and has all the powers and status of the
Land Court and of the Chief Judge thereof in respect of the conduct of
proceedings, maintaining order, contempt of court, citing and representation of
parties interested in the question, summoning witnesses and payment of their
expenses, hearing evidence, awarding costs, amending defects in the
proceedings, and adjourning. The Committee has power to administer oaths to
witnesses in the same manner and to the same ef fect as is conferred upon a court
within the meaning of section 315 of the Cook Islands Act 1915.
(3) (a) All hearings of the Committee must be open to the public provided that
if for any reason the Committee decides that all or any persons be
excluded from the place in which the hearing is held for the whole or any
part of the proceedings, those persons must be excluded accordingly
provided the power conferred by this subsection must not be exercised
for the purpose of excluding any party to the proceedings or any barrister
or solicitor or accredited reporter.
(b) In any case in which the Committee may give any direction under
paragraph (a) and whether or not it gives such a direction the Committee
may make an order forbidding the publication of any report or account of
the whole or any part of the evidence adduced and the breach of any
order made under this subsection or any evasion or attempted evasion of
it is deemed to be contempt of court under the meaning of section 400 of
the Cook Islands Act 1915 and is punishable by the High Court of the
Cook Islands in the ordinary course of its criminal jurisdiction.
(4) The Chief Judge of the Land Court presides at all hearings of the Committee.
(5) The absence of an assessor at any time during a hearing does not invalidate the
proceedings of the Committee provided the number of assessors present is not
reduced below 2.
(6) The decision of the Committee is ﬁnal and may not be questioned in any court.
(7) If at any time the opinions of the Committee are equally divided on any question
the opinion of the Chief Judge prevails.

15 Salary and allowance of members of House of Ariki

18 Languages
(1) All debates and discussions of the House must be conducted in the Maori
language as spoken in Rarotonga and also in the English language.
(2) Every report recommendation or resolution of the House must be in the Maori
language as spoken in Rarotonga and also in the English language:
Provided that the House may by resolution determine that any report,
recommendation or resolution may be in the English language only .
(3) The records of proceedings of the House or in Committees thereof must be in
the English language and such of these records as are speciﬁed in the Standing
Orders of the House must also be in the Maori language as spoken in Rarotonga.
(4) Where there is any conﬂict between the Maori version and the English version
of any report, recommendation, resolution, or record the English version

The kavana, mataiapo and rangatira of the Cook Islands may meet together at

(1) The Koutu Nui may discuss and make recommendations or resolutions on any

votes recorded thereon.

entitled to one vote each.

it thinks ﬁt.

66 Contents of Questions
Every Question shall conform to the following rules—
(a) it shall not include the names of persons or statements not strictly
necessary to render the Question intelligible:
(b) it shall not contain statements which the Member who asks the Question
is not prepared to substantiate:
(c) it shall not contain argument, inferences, opinions, imputations, epithets
or contentious, ironical or of fensive expressions:
(d) it shall not refer to debates or Replies to Questions in the current
Meeting:
(e) it shall not refer to proceedings in a Committee which have not been
reported to the Assembly:
(f) it shall not ask information about any matter which is of its nature secret:
(g) it shall not reﬂect on a decision of a Court of Law and no question shall
be asked on any matter on which a judicial decision is pending:
(h) it shall not be asked for the purpose of obtaining an expression of
opinion, the solution of an abstract legal case or the answer to a
hypothetical proposition:
(i) it shall not be asked as to the character or conduct of any person except
in his or her of f icial or public capacity:
(j) it shall not be asked seeking information readily available in of f icial
publication or ordinary works of reference:
(k) a Question fully answered shall not be asked again during the same
meeting.

Part XXII
Select Committees
160 Dif ferent types of Select Committee
Select Committees may be appointed by the House—
(a) to facilitate the business of the House in Meeting (see Business
Committee, Part XXIII,) or to provide for the convenience and comfort of
Members, or to deal with such other sessional matters as may , in the
opinion of the House, be carried out best in Committee:
(b) to consider a deﬁnite matter of House business arising, such as a
recommendation for the Assembly , and to report back to the House on
such business within a speciﬁed or foreseen time:
(c) to stand indeﬁnitely at the pleasure of the House in the name of a
particular function (such as Customs and Tradition), to receive the House,
from time to time, orders to consider matters falling within the terms of
reference of such Committees and to report back to the House upon such
matters when considered.
161 Appointment of Select Committee
Select Committees shall be appointed from Motion, of which Notice shall be
given; provided however, that if the House, in the course of the proceedings on
a matter, shall decide to refer the same to a Select Committee already appointed
or to be then appointed, such Notice shall not be necessary .
162 Committee to consist of not less than ﬁve or more than ten members
Except as provided in Order 195, no Committee shall consist of less than four
or of more than eight Members, without leave of the House, on Motion with
Notice.
163 Notice of nomination to be given
Every Member intending to move for the appointment of a Select Committee
shall state in his Notice the names of the Members intended to be proposed by
him to be Members of such Committee. Notice shall be required to add any
other Members to such Committee or to substitute a Member for another whose
name has been given Notice of.
164 Member may be discharged from Select Committee
Any Member appointed to the Select Committee may , at any time afterwards,
upon Motion with Notice, be discharged by the House from attending the
Committee, and another Member appointed.
165 Member absent for more than 3 meetings of Committee may forfeit seat on
Committee
If any Member of a Select Committee has absented himself without leave of
absence or, in the opinion of the remaining Members of the Committee, without
good cause from more than three consecutive meetings of such Committee, that
Member shall be deemed to have forfeited his seat on the Committee and an
election to the vacancy so created may be held at the ensuring Meeting of the
House.

175 Absence of quorum at time of meeting
If after the lapse of ten minutes from the time appointed for the meeting of a
