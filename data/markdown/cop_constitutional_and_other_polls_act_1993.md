An Act to provide for the manner in which Constitutional and Other Polls shall be
conducted.
Part 1
Constitutional polls
1 Short title
This Act may be cited as the Constitutional and Other Polls Act 1993.
2 Interpretation
In this Act unless the context otherwise requires—
Constitution means the Constitution of the Cook Islands as set out in the
Schedule 2 to the Cook Islands Constitution Amendment Act 1965 of the
Parliament of New Zealand as amended by Acts of the Legislative Assembly
and Parliament of the Cook Islands
Chief Electoral Of ficermeans the Chief Electoral Of f icer appointed pursuant to
section 3 of the Electoral Act 1966.
3 Constitutional polls
(1) A poll of electors required by Articles 41(2)(b) or 37(7)(b)(ii) of the
Constitution must be conducted in the same manner, with necessary
modiﬁcations, as if it were an election for Members of Parliament, and the
persons entitled to vote in such poll on the same day .
(2) The Queen’s Representative may by Order in Executive Council make
regulations, not inconsistent with the Constitution, prescribing forms and
procedures which may be required for the conduct of any particular poll
required by the Constitution.
(3) The Chief Electoral Of f icer must by warrant report the result of any
constitutional poll to the Speaker of Parliament, and must publicly notify the
result and the date of the warrant.

4 Other polls
(1) Where the Prime Minister with the concurrence of Cabinet determines that any
question or issue ought to be submitted to electors for an expression of public
opinion, the Prime Minister may direct the Chief Electoral Of f icer to conduct a
poll in any one or more electorates as the Prime Minister with the concurrence
of Cabinet may determine, to ascertain the opinion or preferences of electors on
any question or issue.
(2) The Queen’s Representative may by Order in Executive Council make
regulations, not inconsistent with the Constitution, prescribing forms and
procedures which may be required for ascertaining the opinion or preferences of
electors on any question or issue to be submitted to electors under this section.
(3) The persons entitled to express an opinion in any poll conducted under this
section, where the poll is held on the same day as an election, are those who
would be entitled to vote in that election in the electorate or electorates
designated under subsection (1) and, where the poll is held on any other day , are
those persons who were entitled to vote in the electorate or electorates
designated under subsection (1), at the general election preceding the poll.
(4) The Chief Electoral Of f icer must publicly notify the results of any poll
conducted under this section as soon as practicable after the conduct of the poll.
This Act is administered by the Chief Electoral Of f icer.

Pursuant to section 3(2) of the Constitutional and other Polls Regulations 1993, His
Excellency the Queen’s Representative, acting by and with the advice and consent of the
Executive Council, hereby makes the following regulations.
1 Title
These regulations may be cited as the Constitution and Other Polls Regulations
1994.
2 Interpretation
Unless the context otherwise requires—
Act means the Constitutional and Other Polls Act 1993
poll means a poll required to be conducted under the Act
poll day means the day ﬁxed for the conduct of a poll required to be conducted
pursuant to the Act
polling of ficermeans a person appointed under regulation 6
register of votes means the electoral rolls of voters compiled for use for the ﬁrst
general election of members of Parliament of the Cook Islands held after the
coming into force of these regulations
special votes means valid votes cast in advance of polling day by the voters or
votes cast on polling day by voters after the making of a declaration.
3 Application of electoral procedures
(1) Except as expressly provided herein, in the conduct of any poll pursuant to the
Act, the Chief Electoral Of f icer must apply with such modiﬁcations as he or she
deems necessary , the electoral procedures used for the conduct of elections of
members of the Parliament of the Cook Islands.
(2) Where a poll is required to be conducted pursuant to the Act simultaneously
with any general election for members of the Parliament of the Cook Islands,
every Electoral Of f icer appointed under the Electoral Act 1966 is deemed to be
a polling of f icer for the purposes of the poll.
4 Polling districts
In the conduct of any poll pursuant to the Act the boundaries of each polling
district are the electoral boundaries of each Constituency demarcated by the
Cook Islands Constitution for the elections of members of the Parliament of the
Cook Islands.
5 Register of voters
The rolls of voters compiled for use for the election of members of the
Parliament of the Cook Islands are the register of voters to be used by the Chief
Electoral Of f icer in the conduct of any poll pursuant to the Act.
6 Appointment of polling of ficers
The Chief Electoral Of f icer has the power to appoint, upon such terms and
conditions as he or she considers appropriate, such persons as he or she
considers necessary to be polling of f icers for the purpose of conducting a poll
pursuant to the Act.

7 Administration
The Chief Electoral Of f icer is responsible for the ef f icient administration
organisation and conduct of the polling process for any poll required to be
conducted pursuant to the Act.
8 Polling centres
The Chief Electoral Of f icer must—
(a) ﬁx the numbers of polling centres and/or polling booths; and
(b) designate the places for the polling centres and/or polling booths in each
polling district.
9 Public notices
The Chief Electoral Of f icer must issue such public notices as he or she considers
appropriate and necessary for notifying the public of—
(a) the day ﬁxed for the poll:
(b) the purpose or purposes of the poll:
(c) the numbers of polling centres and/or polling booths:
(d) the places designated as polling centres and/or polling booths in each
polling districts:
(e) any other information which the Chief Electoral Of f icer deems necessary .
10 Secrecy of votes
The Chief Electoral Of f icer is responsible for ensuring that every voter exercises
his or her vote in secrecy .
11 V oting method
(1) The procedure for voting at any polling centre and/or polling booth is, subject
to such modiﬁcations as are necessary , the same as that used for voting in the
national general elections of members of the Parliament of the Cook Islands.
(2) The voter must indicate on the voting paper his or her choice, option or
preference by making a mark clearly and directly opposite their choice, option
or preference.
(3) Where a voter’s choice option or preference is not clearly indicated on the
voting paper the Chief Electoral Of f icer must disallow the vote and must not
include it into the total votes for the poll.
(4) When issuing to a voter a polling paper for any poll the following provisions
apply—
(i) the Presiding Of f icer or other authorised of f icer must, before giving the
voter any polling paper, endorse on the counterfoil of the polling paper
the page number and line number where the name of the voter appears in
the register of voters:
(ii) the Presiding Of f icer or other authorised of f icer must rule a line right
through the names and address of the voter in the register of voters to
indicate that the voter has been issued with a polling paper.
12 V oting for overseas polling district
(1) The voting in the overseas polling district in respect of any poll required to be
conducted pursuant to the Act must be conducted by postal vote.
(2) The papers for postal voting must be sent to the address of the voter shown in
the register of voters.

(3) The papers for postal voting must, except where a poll is being conducted
simultaneously with a national general election of members of the Parliament of
the Cook Islands (when the appropriate electoral laws concerning postal voting
apply), be posted to the voter no later than 14 days before the day of the poll.
(4) Every postal vote not received by the Chief Electoral Of f icer by noon on the day
of the poll in the Cook Islands must not be counted in the total votes for the poll.
13 Time for voting
The ordinary voting for any poll must be made between 8 o’clock in the
morning and 6 o’clock in the afternoon.
14 Disallowed votes
(1) The Chief Electoral Of f icer must make the ﬁnal decision on the disallowing of
any vote or any vote disallowed.
(2) Where a voter’s vote is disallowed by any polling of f icer the voter may lodge an
appeal in writing to the Chief Electoral Of f icer and upon receiving such appeal
the Chief Electoral Of f icer must immediately inquire into the appeal and make
a decision thereon and advise the appellant voter of the decision made.
15 Special votes
(1) The Chief Electoral Of f icer may if satisﬁed upon application in writing being
made to him or her, allow a voter to cast their vote in advance of poll day .
(2) The Presiding Of f icer at any polling centre or polling booth must on poll day
issue a voting paper to a voter who is registered on the register of voters of
another polling district, but only after the voter has made and signed a
declaration declaring that he or she is a voter for the polling district on whose
register of voters his or her name appears and that they have not voted
previously for the poll.
(3) Every special vote cast must be despatched or transmitted to the Chief Electoral
Of f icer as soon as practicable after it is made.
16 V oter identiﬁcation
(1) The Presiding Of f icer at any polling centre and/or polling booth may , if they
considers it necessary to conﬁrm the identity of a voter, require the voter to
supply such information as the Presiding Of f icer may require.
(2) Subject to subclause (1) the Presiding Of f icer must issue to the voter the voting
paper.
17 Spoilt votes
Where a voter has spoilt or otherwise damaged a voting paper when making
their vote at a polling centre and/or polling booth that voter is entitled to be
issued another voting paper upon returning to the Presiding Of f icer the spoilt or
damaged voting paper.
18 Remuneration of polling of ficers
The Chief Electoral Of f icer must pay the polling of f icers such rates of
remuneration as he or she considers justiﬁed.
19 V oter to vote only once
(1) A voter may vote only once at a poll.

(2) If a voter has voted more than once all his or her voting papers are disallowed
and must not be included into the total votes for the poll.
20 Counting of votes
(1) Except where the Chief Electoral Of f icer has otherwise authorised, the counting
of the votes for any poll must be carried out under the supervision of the Chief
Electoral Of f icer at the Of f ice of the Chief Electoral Of f icer.
(2) The counting of the votes of the poll must be carried out as soon as possible
after the close of the voting and at such time and place as the Chief Electoral
Of f icer determines.
(3) The counting of the votes must be done by poll district and in each case the
totals for all valid votes cast and disallowed must be recorded and a proper
reconciliation made.
(4) During the counting of votes the following provisions apply—
(i) the Chief Electoral Of f icer must designate such polling of f icers as he or
she considers necessary for the process of the counting of the votes:
(ii) the Chief Electoral Of f icer must cause all containers containing the
voting papers (except those cast pursuant to declarations made) cast at
every polling centre or polling booth to be placed in the sight and
presence of the designated polling of f ices and any other authorised
personnel:
(iii) the Chief Electoral Of f icer must open for inclusion into the counting
process all the envelopes for special votes cast:
(iv) all votes cast for the poll must be checked against the relevant register of
voters:
(v) the counting of votes process must be done in the sight and presence of
such polling of f icers and any other person approved by the Minister in
charge of the Electoral Of f ice.
21 Result of poll
The Chief Electoral Of f icer must publicy notify the results of any poll required
to be carried out under the Act.
22 Forms
(1) The form to be used at the ﬁrst poll held after the coming into force of these
regulations are as set out in the Schedule to these regulations.
(2) The Chief Electoral Of f icer is responsible for the printing issuing and
distribution of all forms required for any poll, and the security of all unused
forms.
(3) Every polling of f icer to whom any forms are issued for a poll is responsible for
their proper care and security and must account to the Chief Electoral Of f icer
when required.
(4) The Chief Electoral Of f icer must as soon as practicable after the poll dispose of
all voting papers used for the poll.

DIRECTIONS
1. This is a poll of persons who are entitled to vote as electors at a general election of
Members of Parliament to determine whether voters agree, or do not agree, to the
Constitution Amendment (No 27) Bill which proposes that the term of Parliament
should be reduced from 5 years to 4 years. The Constitution Amendment (No 27) Bill
is attached to this polling paper.
2. Look carefully at the poll choices, options, or preferences shown on this paper.
3. Then select the one you prefer.
4. Then make a cross or tick or mark clearly in the box directly opposite the choice,
option or preference you selected.
5. Then fold the paper and place it in the container provided.
6. If you spoil this paper in any way , return it to the Presiding Of f icer and obtain another.
7. If you are voting by postal vote, you must do it straightaway and make sure that you
post your vote to the Chief Electoral Of f icer as soon as you have completed it.
KA VEINGA
1. E ikianga na te au tangata tei tika ia kia iki ki roto i te ikianga maata o te au Mema
o te Paramani no te akapapu i te roa o te tuatau o te Paramani me, kia akaiti ia mei
te rima mataiti ki te a mataiti kia tau ki te ture tamanako akatuke anga i te Papa Ture
Metua (No 27) tei kapiti ia ki teia pepa ikianga.
2. Akara meitaki ki te au ikianga e te au manako tei akataka ia ki runga i teia pepa.
3. Kia oti ia koe te akara meitaki, iki i taau i inangaro.
4. Kia oti ia koe te iki i taau i inangaro, e akairo i taau tikoti me kare i taau maka, kia
takataka tikai ki roto i te pia i te pae i te manako taau i iki.
5. Kia oti te akairo anga i taau ikianga, atuatu i teia pepa, e tauru atu ki roto i te vairanga
i akono ia.
6. Me tarevake, me kare, me kino taau akairoanga, akaoki atu i teia pepa ki te tangata
akaaere o te reira ngai ikianga, ma te pati kia oronga akaou ia mai tetai pepa ou naau.
7. Me e ikianga taau na roto i te mere, akaoti viviki koe i taau ikianga e oti tuku vave
atu i te reira ki te Akaaere Maata o te Ikianga.
