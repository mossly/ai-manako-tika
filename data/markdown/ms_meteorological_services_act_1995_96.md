An Act to ensure the provision of meteorological services in the Cook Islands
BE IT ENACTED by the Parliament of the Cook Islands in Session assembled and by
the authority of the same as follows:
1 Short title and commencement
(1) This Act may be cited as the Meteorological Services Act 1995–96.
(2) This Act is deemed to have come into force on a date to be appointed by the
Queen’s Representative by Order in Executive Council.
2 Interpretation
In this Act, unless the context otherwise requires—
Minister means the Minister responsible for meteorological services.
Ministry means the government department from time to time responsible for
the administration of meteorological services.
3 Meteorological services
(1) The Minister must ensure the provision of the meteorological warning service
referred to in section 4.
(2) The Minister must arrange for the provision of—
(a) the making and issuing of forecasts of the weather:
(b) the collection and recording of such meteorological information as is
necessary for the purposes of paragraph (a):
(c) the conduct of meteorological research and investigation:
(d) the furnishing of advice on meteorological matters:
(e) the publication of meteorological information and the results of research
and investigation:
(f) the making and issuing of forecasts of the weather and other
meteorological conditions:
(g) the supply of meteorological information and expertise to the Crown or
any person requiring such information and expertise.
4 Minister to designate meteorological warning service
The Minister must from time to time designate a person who must be
responsible to provide the authorised meteorological warning service in the
Cook Islands.
5 Charge for services
The Ministry must charge for meteorological services provided pursuant to this
Act at a level prescribed by the Minister from time to time.
This Act is administered by the Department of Meteorological Services.
Published under the authority of the Parliament of the Cook Islands.
