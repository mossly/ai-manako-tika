An Act to sanction the expenditure of certain sums of money during the year that ended
31 March 1967.
BE IT ENACTED by the Legislative Assembly of the Cook Islands in Session
assembled, and by the authority of the same, as follows:
1 Short title
This Act may be cited as the Finance Act 1967–68.
[Section 1: amended, on 4 October 1968, by section 2(2) of the Short Titles Amendment Act 1968
(1968 No 6).]
2 Indemnity as to unauthorised expenditure in excess of the statutory limit
Whereas the Minister of Finance has issued and paid during the ﬁnancial year
that ended with 31 March 1967 an amount of £3,685 in excess of the limit
imposed by Article 70(3)(b) of the Cook Islands Constitution as set out in
Schedule 2 to the Cook Islands Constitution Amendment Act 1965 be it
therefore enacted that the application of the said sum is hereby sanctioned.
3 Indemnity as to unauthorised expenditure of £30,844 for the year ended
31 March 1967
Whereas the Minister of Finance has issued under authority of Article 70(3)(b)
of the Cook Islands Constitution as set out in Schedule 2 to the Cook Islands
Constitution Amendment Act 1965 in excess of or without appropriation of the
Cook Islands Legislative Assembly out of the Cook Islands Government
Account the respective sums mentioned in the Schedule to this Act amounting
in the whole to £30,844 and has caused these sums to be applied to the several
services mentioned in the Schedule be it therefore enacted that the application of
the said sums is hereby sanctioned.
4 Repeal
Section 3 of the Appropriation Act 1967 is hereby repealed.

An Act to approve the expenditure of money in excess of the constitutional limit during
the year that ended 31 December 1970.
BE IT ENACTED by the Legislative Assembly of the Cook Islands in Session
assembled, in accordance with the provisions of subclause (1) of Article 41 of the
Constitution of the Cook Islands and by the authority of the same, as follows:
1 Short title
This Act may be cited as the Finance Act 1972.
2 Approval of expenditure in excess of constitutional limit
Whereas the Minister responsible for ﬁnance has issued and paid during the year
that ended on 31 December 1970 an amount of $64,757 in excess of the limit
imposed by Article 70(3)(b) of the Constitution as set out in the Second
Schedule to the Cook Islands Constitution Amendment Act 1965 be it therefore
enacted that the expenditure of the said sum is hereby approved.
This Act is administered by the Ministry of Finance and Economic Management.
Published under the authority of the Parliament of the Cook Islands.

An Act to make provision with respect to public ﬁnance and other matters.
BE IT ENACTED by the Legislative Assembly of the Cook Islands in Session
assembled, and by the authority of the same, as follows:
1 Short title
This Act may be cited as the Finance Act 1973.
2 Cook Islands Government Property Corporation may acquire shares in
Cook Islands Airways Limited
(1) The Cook Islands Government Property Corporation, on behalf of Her
Majesty’s Government in the Cook Islands, is hereby authorised and is deemed
always to have been authorised to subscribe for or otherwise acquire shares in
the capital of Cook Islands Airways Limited a company incorporated in New
Zealand.
(2) All money required to be paid by the Cook Islands Government Property
Corporation in respect of the subscription for or other acquisition of the shares
in the capital of Cook Islands Airways Limited must be paid out of money
appropriated by Parliament for the purpose.
3 Cook Islands Government Property Corporation may acquire shares in
company to be formed to carry on business of establishing hotels
(1) The Cook Islands Government Property Corporation, on behalf of Her
Majesty’s Government in the Cook Islands, is hereby authorised to enter into
agreements with the T ourist Hotel Corporation, a statutory corporation
established in New Zealand, and Air New Zealand Limited, a company
incorporated in New Zealand, in connection with the establishment and business
of a company to be incorporated in New Zealand of which the principal original
members will be the T ourist Hotel Corporation, the Air New Zealand Limited
and the Cook Islands Government Property Corporation, and which has
amongst its objects the business of establishing and conducting hotels.
(2) The Cook Islands Government Property Corporation may from time to time
subscribe for or otherwise acquire shares in the company so to be incorporated
and may from time to time exercise on behalf of Her Majesty’s Government in
the Cook Islands all the rights and powers of the holder of any such shares.
(3) All money required to be paid by the Cook Islands Government Property
Corporation in respect of the subscription for or other acquisition of the shares
in the capital of the company so to be incorporated must be paid out of money
appropriated by Parliament for the purpose.
4 Cook Islands Government Property Corporation may acquire shares in
company to be formed to carry on business of shipping
(1) The Cook Islands Government Property Corporation, on behalf of Her
Majesty’s Government in the Cook Islands, is hereby authorised to enter into
agreements with the New Zealand Shipping Corporation, a statutory corporation
established in New Zealand in connection with the establishment and business
of a company to be incorporated in New Zealand of which the original members
will be the New Zealand Shipping Corporation, and the Cook Islands
