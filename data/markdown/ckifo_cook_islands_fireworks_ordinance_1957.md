An Ordinance made by the High Commissioner with the advice and consent of the
Legislative Council of the Cook Islands and in pursuance of the Cook Islands Amendment
Act 1946, to make provision for the control of the manufacture, importation and use of
ﬁreworks.
[Preamble: amended, on 4 August 1965, by section 57(3) of the Cook Islands Amendment Act 1964 (1964
No 70) (NZ).]
1 Short title
This Ordinance may be cited as the Cook Islands Fireworks Ordinance 1957.
2 Interpretation
(1) In this Ordinance unless the context otherwise requires,—
ﬁreworks means any explosive and any ﬁrework composition enclosed in any
case or contrivance or otherwise manufactured so as to form a squib cracker,
serpent, rocket (other than ship safety rocket), maroon, lance wheel, Chinese
ﬁre, Roman candle or other article made or adapted for the production of
pyrotechnic ef fects and includes sparkler, matches and throw-downs
import means to bring or cause to be brought or sent or to introduce in any
other way whatever into the Cook Islands.
(2) In this Ordinance, terms deﬁned by the Cook Islands Act 1915 shall, subject to
the provisions of subsection (1) hereof, have the meanings thereby assigned to
them.
3 Fireworks not to be made or constructed
No person in the Cook Islands shall construct or manufacture any ﬁreworks or
produce ﬁreworks in any manner or by any method whatever whether by
making a new article or adapting any existing article.
4 Importation prohibited without consent and Police or Customs may inspect
packages
(1) No person shall import any ﬁreworks without having ﬁrst obtained the written
consent of the High Commissioner.
(2) Any of f icer of Police or Customs may detain and examine the contents of any
imported package in respect whereof a breach of this section is reasonably
suspected and any ﬁreworks found to be imported contrary to the provisions of
this section shall be seized.
[Clause 4(1): amended, on 4 August 1965, by section 57(3) of the Cook Islands Amendment Act
1964 (1964 No 70) (NZ).]
5 Possession only with written consent of High Commissioner
(1) No person shall have in his possession any ﬁreworks, whether purchased or
acquired in any other way whatever, except pursuant to the written consent of
the High Commissioner.
(2) In proceedings for any breach of this section the burden of proving the existence
and terms of any such consent shall lie on the accused.
(3) The occupier of any house in which any ﬁreworks are found shall, in the
absence of proof to the contrary , be deemed to be in possession of such
ﬁreworks.
[Clause 5: amended, on 4 August 1965, by section 57(3) of the Cook Islands Amendment Act 1964
(1964 No 70) (NZ).]

6 High Commissioner may impose conditions and delegate his powers
(1) Any consent granted by the High Commissioner under this Ordinance may be
given subject to such terms and conditions as the High Commissioner, in his
discretion, may impose.
(2) The High Commissioner may delegate his functions under this Ordinance to any
Resident Agent or of f icer of Police or Customs and any functions so delegated
shall be exercised subject to any general or speciﬁc directions which may be
given by the High Commissioner in that behalf.
[Clause 6: amended, on 4 August 1965, by section 57(3) of the Cook Islands Amendment Act 1964
(1964 No 70) (NZ).]
7 Police may be authorised to enter , search and if necessary seize
Any of f icer under of Police, on being authorised so to do by warrant under the
hand of a Judge or Commissioner or Registrar of the High Court, may , by force
if need be, enter and search any land or buildings in or upon which he has
reasonable cause to believe that there are any ﬁreworks in respect of which a
breach of this Ordinance has been committed, and any such ﬁreworks found on
such land or buildings shall be seized.
8 Fireworks seized to become the property of the Crown
Any ﬁreworks seized pursuant to any provision of this Ordinance shall become
the property of Her Majesty free and discharged from all rights, title, estate or
interest possessed in respect thereof by any other person and shall be destroyed
or otherwise disposed of as in any case the High Commissioner may direct.
[Clause 8: amended, on 4 August 1965, by section 57(3) of the Cook Islands Amendment Act 1964
(1964 No 70) (NZ).]
9 Of fences
Every person commits a breach of this Ordinance who obstructs any of f icer of
Police or Customs in the lawful exercise of any right of detention, examination,
search, seizure or any other right under this Ordinance.
10 Breaches
Every person commits a breach of this Ordinance who sets of f any ﬁreworks—
(a) in or on any public place or so near thereto as to endanger, annoy , or
frighten any person:
(b) in such a place or so situated that he knows that any property whether
movable or immovable is likely to catch ﬁre therefrom.
11 Penalties
Every person is liable to imprisonment not exceeding 3 months or a ﬁne not
exceeding 100 pounds who, with intent to do any bodily harm to any person,
sets of f any ﬁreworks in any place whatever or sends or delivers to or causes to
be received by any person any ﬁreworks.
12 Fine
Every person who commits a breach of this Ordinance for which no speciﬁc
penalty is elsewhere provided, shall be liable to a ﬁne not exceeding
100 pounds.
This Ordinance is administered by the Minister of Customs.

An Ordinance to prohibit the importation of Fireworks into the Island of Rarotonga.
1 Short title and commencement
This Ordinance may be cited as the Rarotonga Fireworks Ordinance 1957, and
shall come into force on the day of assent by the High Commissioner.
[Clause 1: amended, on 4 August 1965, by section 57(3) of the Cook Islands Amendment Act 1964
(1964 No 70) (NZ).]
2 Interpretation
In this Ordinance Fireworks means any explosive and any ﬁrework
composition when such explosive or composition is enclosed in any case or
contrivance, or is otherwise manufactured so as to form a squib, cracker,
serpent, rocket (other than ship safety rocket), maroon, lance wheel, Chinese
ﬁre, Roman candle or other article specially adapted for the production of
pyrotechnic ef fects and includes sparkler matches and throw downs.
3 Unlawful to import
It shall not be lawful for any person to import Fireworks into the Island of
Rarotonga.
4 Imported Fireworks may be seized
(1) Any of f icer of police may , on being authorised so to do by warrant under the
hand of a Judge or Registrar of the High Court, seize and take possession of any
Fireworks imported into Rarotonga and for that purpose may enter into or upon
any land or building in or upon which such Fireworks may be, and in so doing
may use such force, if any , as may be necessary .
(2) All Fireworks seized under this section shall become the property of Her
Majesty the Queen free and discharged from all right, title, estate or interest
possessed in respect thereof by any other person.
5 Penalty
Every person who imports Fireworks into Rarotonga after this Ordinance comes
into force shall be guilty of an of fence and shall be liable to a ﬁne of 50 pounds.
This Ordinance is administered by the Revenue Management Divison of the Ministry of
Finance and Economic Management.
Published under the authority of the Parliament of the Cook Islands.
