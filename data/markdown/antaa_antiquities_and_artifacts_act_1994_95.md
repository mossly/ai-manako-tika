An Act to provide for the better protection of antiquities, to establish and record the
ownership of Cook Islands artifacts, and to control the sale of artifacts within the Cook
Islands.
BE IT ENACTED by the Parliament of the Cook Islands in Session assembled, and by
the authority of the same as follows:
1 Short title
This Act may be cited as the Antiquities and Artifacts Act 1994–95.
2 Interpretation
In this Act, unless the context otherwise requires,—
antiquity means—
(a) any chattel of any kind whatsoever, not being a chattel to which any of
paragraphs (b) to (h) of this deﬁnition applies, which—
(i) is of national, historical, scientiﬁc, or artistic importance; and
(ii) relates to the European discovery , settlement, or development of
the Cook Islands; and
(iii) is, or appears to be, more than 60 years old:
(b) any artifact:
(c) any book, diary , letter, document, paper, record, or other written matter
(whether in manuscript or printed form), photographic negative or
printing, ﬁlm, printed reproduction of any picture, or sound recording—
(i) which relates to the Cook Islands and is of national, historical,
scientiﬁc, artistic, or literary importance; and
(ii) which is more than 60 years old; and
(iii) of which, in the case of a book ﬁrst printed and published in the
Cook Islands, no copy is in the custody of the Cook Islands
National Library:
(d) any work of art which relates to the Cook Islands and which is more than
60 years old, and is of national, historical, or artistic value or importance:
(e) any type specimen of any animal, plant, or mineral existing or formerly
existing in the Cook Islands:
(f) any meteorite or part of a meteorite recovered in the Cook Islands:
(g) any bones, feathers, or other parts or the eggs of species of animals, birds,
reptiles, or amphibians native to the Cook Islands which are generally
believed to be extinct:
(h) any ship, boat, or aircraft, or any part of any ship, boat, or aircraft, or any
equipment, cargo, or article belonging to any ship, boat, or aircraft in any
case where that ship, boat, or aircraft has been, or appears to have been,
a wreck in the Cook Islands, or within the territorial waters of the Cook
Islands, for more than 60 years and that ship, boat, aircraft, equipment,
cargo, or article as the case may be, is of national, historical, scientiﬁc, or
artistic value or importance
artifact means any chattel, carving, object, or thing which relates to the history ,
art, culture, traditions, or economy of the Cook Islands Maori or other

3 Act binds the Crown
This Act binds the Crown.
4 Minister may acquire antiquities
(1) The Minister—
(a) may with the approval of Cabinet purchase or otherwise acquire; or
(b) accept by way of gift, bequest, or otherwise,
any antiquity .
(2) Any antiquity acquired under this section must be kept in safe custody in
accordance with the directions of the Secretary .
5 Restrictions on export of antiquities
(1) It is not lawful after the commencement of this Act for any person to remove or
attempt to remove any antiquity from the Cook Islands otherwise than pursuant
to the authority and in conformity with the terms and conditions of a written
certiﬁcate of permission given by the Secretary under this Act:
Provided that the Secretary may from time to time, by notice in the Gazette,
exempt any class or classes of antiquities from the provisions of this section
where satisﬁed that—
(a) suf f icient examples of that class or those classes are held in public
ownership in the Cook Islands; and
(b) it would not be contrary to the public interest to exempt that class or
those classes.
(2) Every person who, without reasonable excuse contravenes any provision of this
section or of the terms and conditions of a written certiﬁcate of permission given
pursuant to subsection (1) commits an of fence, and is liable on conviction to a
ﬁne not exceeding $1,000.
(3) Nothing in this section applies to any antiquity lawfully taken and ordinarily
kept outside of the Cook Islands.
6 Application for permission
(1) Every application for permission to remove an antiquity from the Cook Islands
must be in the form provided by the Secretary .
(2) The Secretary , in considering any application under this section, in respect of
the antiquity sought to be removed, must have regard to—
(a) its historical, archaeological, scientiﬁc, cultural, literary , artistic, or other
special national or local importance; and
(b) its spiritual or emotional association with the people of the Cook Islands
or any group or section thereof; and
(c) its rarity; and
(d) the extent to which similar articles are held in public ownership in the
Cook Islands; and
(e) the probable ef fect of its removal on historical or scientiﬁc study or
research in the Cook Islands; and
(f) any other matters which appear to him or her to be relevant.
(3) After having regard to the matters referred to in subsection (2) and after making
such inquiries and investigations and seeking such expert opinion as he or she

thinks ﬁt, the Secretary may grant permission, either unconditionally or subject
to such terms and conditions as may be imposed by him or her in writing, for the
removal of the antiquity from the Cook Islands:
Provided that the Secretary must refuse to grant permission unless satisﬁed that
the removal of the antiquity—
(a) would not be to the substantial detriment of historical or scientiﬁc study

(b) would not be contrary to the public interest.
7 Certiﬁcate of permission
Where permission is granted by the Secretary for the removal of any antiquity
from the Cook Islands, a certiﬁcate must be issued to the applicant under the
hand of the Secretary .
8 Conditions imposed by Secretary
(1) Without prejudice to the generality of the authority given under this Act to
impose terms and conditions, the Secretary may , when granting permission to
remove any antiquity from the Cook Islands, impose conditions—
(a) requiring the owner to permit the antiquity to be copied by photography ,
cast, or otherwise in such manner, in such numbers, and by such person,
as the Secretary may direct:
(b) requiring the owner of the antiquity to deliver it to such person as the
Secretary may direct for the purpose of being packed and dispatched
from the Cook Islands, at a cost and risk of the owner, to the address
speciﬁed by the owner:
(c) requiring the antiquity to be returned to the Cook Islands no later than the
date of the expiry of a period speciﬁed in the certiﬁcate of permission to
remove the antiquity from the Cook Islands, and to require the deposit of
a bond of such amount as the Secretary thinks ﬁt, pending its return.
(2) Every copy made pursuant to subsection (1)(a), subject to the provisions of the
law for the time being in force in the Cook Islands relating to copyright, is the
property of the Crown and must be kept in safe custody in accordance with the
directions of the Secretary .
9 Appeal against Secretary’s decision
(1) Where the Secretary has refused permission to the removal of any antiquity or
has granted any such permission subject to terms and conditions, the applicant
for the permission may appeal to the Minister on the grounds that, having regard
to the provisions of this Act, the decision of the Secretary was not reasonably
arrived at, and the Minister may , after seeking such advice (if any) the Minister
considers necessary , conﬁrm, vary , or reverse the decision of the Secretary , and
the decision of the Minister with regard to any such appeal is ﬁnal.
(2) Any appeal under this section must be forwarded to the Minister within 21 days
after the date when the decision of the Secretary is made known to the appellant.

11 Establishing ownership and custody of artifacts
(1) Any artifact found anywhere in the Cook Islands or within the territorial waters
of the Cook Islands after the commencement of this Act is deemed to be prima
facie the property of the Crown:
Provided that where any artifact has been recovered from the grave of any
person or persons whose identity is known, the matter must be referred to the
High Court to determine who is the proper person or who are the proper persons
to hold custody of the artifact.
(2) Despite the provisions of subsection (1), if actual or traditional ownership,
rightful possession, or custody of any artifact referred to in that subsection is
subsequently claimed, the Minister or any person who may have any right, title,
estate, or interest in any such artifact may apply to the court to exercise any part
of its jurisdiction under section 12:
Provided that no right, title, estate, or interest in any such artifact exists or is
deemed to exist solely by virtue of ownership of the land from which the artifact
was found or recovered.
(3) Every person who, after the commencement of this Act, ﬁnds any artifact
anywhere in the Cook Islands or within the territorial waters of the Cook Islands
must, within 28 days of ﬁnding the artifact, notify either the Secretary , or the
nearest Government representative who must notify the Secretary of the ﬁnding
of the artifact:
Provided that in the case of any artifact found during the course of any
archaeological investigation, the notiﬁcation must be made within 28 days of the
completion of the ﬁeld work undertaken in connection with the investigation.
(4) Upon receipt of a notiﬁcation in accordance with subsection (3) the Secretary
must take such action as the Secretary deems appropriate to provide for the
examination of the artifact, its recording and its custody , either by the ﬁnder or
otherwise, and on such conditions as the Secretary deems ﬁt.
(5) Every person who ﬁnds any artifact, knowing or having reasonable cause to
suspect that it is an artifact, and contravenes subsection (3) commits an of fence
and is liable on conviction to a ﬁne not exceeding $1,000.

13 Disposal of artifacts
(1) It is not lawful after the commencement of this Act for any person to sell or
otherwise dispose of any artifact, knowing or having reasonable cause to suspect
that it is an artifact, otherwise than to a registered collector or to the Secretary
or through the of f ices of a licensed auctioneer or a licensed secondhand dealer:
Provided that this subsection does not apply to any disposition by any person to
a relative of that person, whether by way of gift inter vivos , or pursuant to a
testamentary disposition, or under the intestacy of that person, or by
survivorship on the death of that person.
(2) Despite anything in subsection (12), no person or institution entrusted with the
custody of any artifact in accordance with the terms of section 11(4) may
dispose of that artifact other than at the direction of the Secretary .
(3) Without limiting in any way the Secretary’s powers under subsection (2), the
Secretary may , on application and subject to such conditions as he or she
considers necessary , authorise approved institutions to employ destructive
analytical techniques to speciﬁed artifacts or to speciﬁed classes of artifacts.
(4) Every person, other than a registered collector, who contravenes the provisions
of this section commits an of fence, and is liable on conviction to a ﬁne not
exceeding $1,000.
(5) On the conviction of any person of an of fence against this section in respect of
the disposition of any artifact, the court may , if it thinks ﬁt, declare the artifact
to be forfeited to the Crown.

14 Registration of collectors
(1) No collector may add or attempt to add to his or her collection of artifacts and
no person may be granted custody of any artifact by the Secretary unless the
collector or person has been registered as a collector of artifacts by the
Secretary .
(2) The Secretary has, where reasonable grounds for doing so exist, power to refuse
registration and in particular is not required to register as a collector of artifacts
any person—
(a) who is not ordinarily resident in the Cook Islands; or
(b) who has been convicted of an of fence against provision of this Act or of
any other enactment for the time being in force relating to historic or
cultural places.
(3) It is a condition of registration of any person as a registered collector that—
(a) except in the case of a gift or bequest to a relative of that person, disposal
of part or the whole of his or her collection must be only to another
registered collector, or to the Secretary , or through the of f ices of a
licensed auctioneer or a licensed secondhand dealer:
(b) his or her collection must be made available for examination at any
reasonable time by any person so authorised by the Secretary:
(c) he or she will undertake to notify the Secretary of any change in his or
her collection which has taken place otherwise than through the of f ices of
a licensed auctioneer or a licensed secondhand dealer within 14 days of
such change:
(d) he or she will notify the Secretary of any change in the place at which the
collection is held within 14 days after the date of the change.
(4) Every application for registration as a collector of artifacts must be in the form
provided by the Secretary , and, where registration is granted by the Secretary to
any person, a certiﬁcate of registration must be issued to the applicant under the
hand of the Secretary .
(5) The Secretary must revoke the registration of any registered collector who is
convicted of an of fence against this Act or any Act for the time being in force
relating to historic or cultural places.
(6) Every person who—
(a) not being a registered collector,—
(i) acquires or attempts to acquire any artifact, knowing or having
reasonable cause to suspect that it is an artifact, otherwise than in
accordance with any provision of this Act; or
(ii) falsely declares to any person that he or she is a registered
collector; and
(b) being a registered collector, fails to comply with any of the conditions of
registration speciﬁed in subsection (3):
commits an of fence and is liable on conviction to a ﬁne not exceeding $1,000.

16 Certiﬁcate of examination to be issued by Secretary
(1) At the request of a licensed auctioneer or of a licensed secondhand dealer, or at
the request of any registered collector, the Secretary , after obtaining such
independent expert advice (if any) that he or she deems necessary , must issue a
certiﬁcate of examination in respect of any artifact within 28 days of the artifact
being ﬁrst made available for examination.
(2) The Secretary upon being requested to issue a certiﬁcate of examination by a
licensed auctioneer or a licensed secondhand dealer, must notify the Minister
availability of the artifact and subject to the approval of the Minister of Finance,
the Minister may make an of fer for its purchase.
(3) Every certiﬁcate of examination issued by the Secretary in respect of any
artifact must be in the form provided by the Secretary and must be numbered,
and every artifact examined by the Secretary for the purpose of issuing a
certiﬁcate of examination must be clearly identiﬁed with a registration number.
(4) Every person who—
(a) knowingly removes or alters the registration number identifying any
artifact pursuant to this section; or
(b) knowingly alters a certiﬁcate of examination issued by the Secretary in
respect of any artifact; or
(c) makes or causes to be made any certiﬁcate of examination purporting to
be issued by the Secretary without the authority of the Secretary—
commits an of fence, and is liable on conviction to a ﬁne not exceeding $500.
17 Disclosure of information
(1) No person who under this Act becomes aware of the ownership or location of
any artifact may disclose any information relating thereto to any other person
except as required or permitted pursuant to this Act, or with the written consent
of the Secretary or of the owner of the artifact, or of the person having lawful
custody thereof.
(2) Any person who knowingly acts in contravention of subsection (1) commits an
of fence and is liable on conviction to a ﬁne not exceeding $500.
18 Replica of artifact
(1) Every person who manufactures for sale a replica of any artifact is required to
identify it clearly and permanently as such.
(2) Every person who, without reasonable cause, fails to comply with the provisions
of subsection (1) commits an of fence and is liable on conviction to a ﬁne not
exceeding $500.
19 Expenses of administration
All expenses incurred in the administration of this Act must be paid out of such
money as may from time to time be appropriated by Parliament for the purpose.

20 Annual report
The Secretary must include in his or her report to the Minister for every
ﬁnancial year an account of the operation of this Act, with particular reference
to the artifacts found, the artifacts placed in the custody of other persons
(whether by the Secretary or by the High Court) and the permission given by the
Secretary for the removal of antiquities from the Cook Islands.
21 Saving of other enactments
The provisions of this Act are in addition to and not in substitution for the
provisions of any other enactment but, except where this Act otherwise
provides, nothing in this Act derogates from any provision of any other
enactment. Compliance with the provisions of any other enactment does not
confer any relief or exemption from liability under this Act, but no person may
be convicted of any of fence under this Act in respect of the same matter for
which he or she has been convicted of an of fence under any other enactment.
22 Regulations
The Queen’s Representative may from time to time by Order in Executive
Council, make regulations providing for such matters as are contemplated by or
necessary for giving full ef fect to the provisions of this Act and for the due
administration thereof.
23 Consequential amendments
The Cook Islands Amendment Act 1950 is amended, by repealing Part 1.
This Act is administered by the Ministry of Cultural Development.
