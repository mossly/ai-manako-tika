An Act to establish a national awards system in the Cook Islands.
The Parliament of the Cook Islands enacts as follows—
1 Title
This Act is the T e T ango Raumaire o A vaiki Act 2025.
2 Commencement
This Act comes into force on the day after the date on which it is assented to by
the King’s Representative.
Part 1
Preliminary matters
3 Purpose
The purpose of this Act is to establish a national awards system for the Cook
Islands that:
(a) recognises service, excellence, and innovation by acknowledging and
celebrating outstanding achievements in ﬁelds such as arts, sciences,
literature, sports, and humanitarian work, to honour individuals and to
inspire others to strive for greatness:
(b) fosters national pride and unity by celebrating accomplishments that
contribute positively to the nation’s development and reputation and by
allowing the persons who are honoured to serve as symbols of national
achievement and solidarity .
4 Interpretation
In this Act, unless the context otherwise requires,—
Cook Islander has the meaning given in section 21 of the Cook Islands
Immigration Act 2021
excellence means the quality of being extremely good and contributing over and
above in their area
innovation means the introduction of new things, ideas, or ways of doing
something in a particular area of work
permanent resident has the meaning given in section 34 of the Cook Islands
Immigration Act 2021 (and, to avoid doubt, does not include honorary
permanent residents)
Selection Committee means the committee established under section 14
visitor means a person who is not a Cook Islander or permanent resident and
who is normally resident in an overseas country .
5 General rules for eligibility for awards
(1) Except as provided in sections 11 and 12, a person is eligible for an award if—
(a) they are—
(i) a Cook Islander; or
(ii) a permanent resident; and
(b) they are normally resident in the Cook Islands and are resident on the
date they are nominated for an award.

6 T ui o te Mira Nui o A vaiki and T apairu o te Mira Nui o A vaiki
(1) The following 2 awards may be awarded to 1 recipient for each award every
6 years:
(a) Tui o te Mira Nui o A vaiki award (for men):
(b) T apairu o te Mira Nui o A vaiki award (for women).
(2) These awards will recognise exemplary and outstanding service, leadership, and

7 Akatauira o te Mira Nui o A vaiki
(1) The Akatauira o te Mira Nui o A vaiki award may be awarded to 1 recipient
every 2 years.
(2) The award will recognise exceptional service, leadership, and achievement.
8 Pu ¯ tokotoko o te Mira Nui o A vaiki
(1) The Pu ¯ tokotoko o te Mira Nui o A vaiki award may be awarded to 1 recipient
every 2 years.
(2) The award will recognise commendable service, leadership, and achievement.
Subpart 2—Premier awards
9 Rei A ¯ rangatu ¯ o A vaiki (Public Service Medal)
(1) The Rei A ¯ rangatu ¯ o A vaiki award may be awarded to 1 recipient every 2 years.
(2) The award will recognise outstanding contributions to the work of the Public
Service, Crown agencies, and State-owned enterprises.
(3) The Public Service Commissioner may nominate 1 or more persons who they
are satisﬁed meet that criterion to the Selection Committee.
10 Ētu ¯ Matariki o A vaiki (Star of Excellence)
(1) The Ētu ¯ Matariki o A vaiki award may be awarded to not more than 2 recipients
every 2 years.
(2) The award will recognise an outstanding contribution to 1 or more of the
following:
(a) social impact and sports:
(b) arts, culture, and the humanities:
(c) business, science, and innovation:
(d) environment and conservation.
(3) Further criteria or other details for the subject areas in subsection (2)(a) to (d)
may be—
(a) set by the Cabinet:
(b) prescribed in regulations.

12 T aiki Pa ¯ eru o T e-Atukura (Leadership Mace)
(1) The T aiki Pa ¯eru o T e-Atukura award may be awarded by the Prime Minister at
any time without limit on the number of awards to recipients to acknowledge,
recognise, and reward extraordinary achievements not covered by the awards set
out in sections 6 to 11.
(2) Despite section 5(1), this award may be conferred on—
(a) a person described in section 5(1)(a) who is not normally resident in the
Cook Islands; or
(b) a person who is a visitor.
(3) T o avoid doubt, section 5(2) and (3) applies to this award.

Part 3
Administration
14 Selection Committee
(1) A Selection Committee must be established by the Cabinet.
(2) The committee must—
(a) assess and screen all persons nominated to receive awards; and
(b) recommend to the Cabinet names of persons to receive awards.
(3) Subsection (2) does not apply to the T aiki Pa ¯eru o T e-Atukura (Leadership
Mace) award ( see section 16(2)).
(4) The Cabinet may appoint up to 5 committee members.
(5) Except as provided in section 19, the committee may determine its own
procedure.
15 Approval by Cabinet
Except as provided in section 16(2), the conferring of any award requires the
approval of the Cabinet.
16 Procedure for conferral of T aiki Pa ¯ eru o T e-Atukura (Leadership Mace)
(1) The names of the person or persons who the Prime Minister decides will receive
the T aiki Pa ¯eru o T e-Atukura (Leadership Mace) award must be notiﬁed to the
Cabinet.
(2) The Prime Minister’s decision under this section is not subject to review by the
Selection Committee or Cabinet.
17 Members’ expenses and support of Selection Committee
(1) Members of the committee must not be paid any remuneration other than the
reimbursement of expenses associated with their work on the committee that are
approved by the committee.
(2) The Of f ice of the Prime Minister through its Cabinet Services division must
support the meetings of the committee by payment of those expenses up to $100
per member per meeting.
(3) However, a member of the committee who is also an employee, an of f icer, a
head of department, or the Commissioner, under the Public Service Act 2009, is
not eligible to receive any payment of expenses for their work on the committee.
18 Eligibility criteria and merit
(1) In addition to meeting the requirements in section 5, awards must be conferred
based on whether a person meets the eligibility criteria for a particular award set
out in sections 6 to 12.
(2) The eligibility criteria must be assessed on merit only and not on other factors,
for example, geographical distribution.
19 Procedure
(1) The Selection Committee—

Part 4
General and miscellaneous provisions
21 A wards forfeited for serious or gross misconduct
(1) A person who receives an award under this Act and who the Cabinet is satisﬁed
is guilty of serious or gross misconduct forfeits that award.
(2) It is immaterial whether—
(a) the conduct occurred before or after the conferring of the award on the
person:
(b) the person is deceased.
(3) The decision of the Cabinet under this section may be appealed to the High
Court by the following persons:
(a) the person whose award is forfeited under this section:
(b) in the case of an award that is forfeited posthumously , the spouse, child,
or grandchild of the person, or another person with a material connection
to the person.
22 Of fences
(1) Sections 287, 289, 290, and 302 of the Crimes Act 1969 apply with any
necessary modiﬁcations but the Court may impose a ﬁne only on conviction of
an individual, not exceeding $5,000.
(2) It is an of fence for a person to falsely represent that they are the recipient of an
award under this Act and on conviction an individual is liable to a ﬁne not
exceeding $5,000.
23 Regulations
The King’s Representative may , by Order in Executive Council, make
regulations for all or any of the following purposes:
(a) prescribing forms to be used for the purposes of this Act:
(b) prescribing criteria or other details for the subject areas set out in
section 10(2)(a) to (d):
(c) providing, under section 19(1)(b), a date for the committee to ﬁnalise its
recommendations:
(d) providing for any other matters contemplated by this Act, necessary for
its full administration, or necessary for giving it full ef fect.
24 T ransitional provision
Section 19(1) does not apply in 2025.
This Act is administered by the Of f ice of the Prime Minister.
