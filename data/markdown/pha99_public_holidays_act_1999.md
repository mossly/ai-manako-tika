An Act to establish certain days as public holidays in the Cook Islands.
BE IT ENACTED by the Parliament of the Cook Islands in Session assembled, and by
the authority of the same as follows:
1 Short title
This Act may be cited as the Public Holidays Act 1999.
2 Interpretation
In this Act—
Anzac Day means 25 April in each year
Boxing Day means 26 December in each year
Christmas Day means 25 December in each year
Constitution Day means 4 August in each year
Cook Islands Gospel Day means 26 October in each year
Easter Monday is the same date in each year as Easter Monday is observed in
New Zealand
Good Friday is the same date in each year as Good Friday is observed in New
Zealand
New Y ear’s day means 1 January in each year
Ra o te Ui Ariki means the ﬁrst Friday of July in each calendar year
Sovereign’s Birthday is the same date as the reigning Sovereign’s birthday is
observed in New Zealand
the Day Following New Y ear’s day means 2 January in each year.
[Section 2 Ra o te Ui Ariki: replaced, on 11 June 2013, by section 4 of the Public Holidays (Ra o
te Ui Ariki) Amendment Act 2013 (2013 No 4).]
3 Days to be observed as public holidays throughout Cook Islands
(1) The days set out in Schedule 1 must be observed as public holidays throughout
the Cook Islands.
(2) Where a public holiday (other than a Sunday and Anzac Day) falls on a Saturday
or a Sunday , the holiday must be observed on the following Monday . If both
Christmas Day , Boxing Day , New Y ear’s day and the Day Following New
Y ear’s Day fall on a Saturday and a Sunday , then those public holidays must be
observed respectively on the next Monday and Tuesday .
