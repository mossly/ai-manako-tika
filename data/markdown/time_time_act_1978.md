An Act to consolidate the law relating to ﬁxing the time for general purposes in the Cook
Islands.
BE IT ENACTED by the Legislative Assembly of the Cook Islands in session
assembled, and by the authority of the same, as follows—
1 Short title
This Act may be cited as the Time Act 1978.
2 Cook Islands Standard Time
For the purposes of this Act and of any other Act the expression Cook Islands
Standard Time means the time corresponding to the longitude of 150°W of
Greenwich, being 10 hours in arrears of Greenwich mean time.
[Section 2: amended, on 11 June 1979, by section 2 of the Time Amendment Act 1978–79 (1978–79
No 24).]
3 Time for general purposes
Except as provided by section 4, the time for general purposes in the Cook
Islands is Cook Islands Standard Time.
4 Cook Islands Daylight Time
(1) The Queen’s Representative may from time to time by Order in Executive
Council, declare that for such period or periods as may be prescribed in the
order, the time for general purposes in the Cook Islands is a period up to 1 hour
in advance of Cook Islands Standard Time as the Queen’s Representative
directs.
(2) Any period prescribed in any order under subsection (1) commences at 2 am
Cook Islands Standard Time on the day , being a Sunday , speciﬁed in the order,
and ceases at 2 am Cook Islands Standard Time on the day , being a Sunday ,
speciﬁed in the order or in any subsequent order.
(3) The period or periods prescribed in any order under subsection (1), may be for
either the whole or part of any year.
(4) During any period that the time for general purposes in the Cook Islands is
determined under the foregoing provisions of this section, that time is known as
Cook Islands Daylight Time.
[Section 4(1): amended, on 11 June 1979, by section 3 of the Time Amendment Act 1978–79
(1978–79 No 24); on 6 April 1982, by section 4 of the Constitution Amendment (No 10)
Act 1981–82 (1981–82 No 20).]
5 Ef fect of commencement and cessation of Cook Islands Daylight Time on
pay and allowances
(1) Where, by reason of the commencement of Cook Islands Daylight Time on the
day speciﬁed in any order under section 4(1), the hours worked by any person
on that day are less than the hours he or she would otherwise have worked, the
pay and allowances of that person for those hours is an amount equal to the
amount of the pay and allowances for the hours the person would otherwise
have worked.
(2) Where, by reason of the cessation of Cook Islands Daylight Time on the day
speciﬁed in any order under section 4(1), the hours worked by any person on
that day are in excess of the hours he or she would otherwise have worked, the

6 Reference to point of time in enactments, Orders in Executive Council, etc
Wherever any reference to a point of time occurs in any enactment, Order in
Executive Council, order, regulation, rule, by-law , deed, notice or other
document whatsoever, the time referred to is, unless it is otherwise speciﬁcally
stated, be deemed to be the time for general purposes in the Cook Islands as
determined under section 3 or, as the case may be, section 4.
This Act is administered by the Premier.
Published under the authority of the Parliament of the Cook Islands.
