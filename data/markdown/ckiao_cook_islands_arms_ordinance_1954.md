An Ordinance made by the High Commissioner with the advice and consent of the
Legislative Council of the Cook Islands and in pursuance of the Cook Islands Amendment
Act 1946 to make provision for the better control of arms and explosives in the Cook
Islands.
[Preamble: amended, on 7 June 1965, by section 3(4) of the Cook Islands Amendment Act 1965 (1965 No 1).]
1 Short Title
This Ordinance may be cited as the Cook Islands Arms Ordinance 1954.
2 Deﬁnitions
In this Ordinance, unless the context otherwise requires,—
explosive includes any article of which an explosive forms part and which is
capable of destructive ef fect by way of explosion
ﬁrearm includes any weapon from which a missile is discharged by the force of
any explosive substance or by compressed air
owner in relation to ﬁrearms, includes any person for the time being in
possession thereof
pistol means any ﬁrearm the barrel of which, whether cut down or not, does not
exceed 18 inches in length and includes automatics, machine pistols and
sub-machine guns.
[Section 2 pistol: inserted, on 1 January 1955, by section 3 of the Arms Amendment Ordinance 1955
(1955 No 2).]
3 Carriage or possession of Arms, Ammunition or Explosives
(1) No person shall carry or be in possession of any ﬁrearm, ammunition, or
explosive except for some lawful, proper and suf f icient purpose.
(2) In proceedings for an of fence against this section the burden of proving the
existence of such a purpose shall lie upon the accused.
4 Introduction of Arms and Ammunition into Cook Islands
(1) Except as provided in section 5, no person shall bring or cause to be brought or
sent into the Cook Islands any ﬁrearm, ammunition, or explosive save in
pursuance of a permit issued to him for that purpose by the High Commissioner.
(2) In proceedings for an of fence against this section the burden of proving the
existence and terms of such a permit as aforesaid shall lie on the accused.
(3) Any permit issued under this section may be at any time revoked by the High
Commissioner or any other person appointed by him.
(4) If any constable or any of f icer of Customs has reasonable grounds to suspect
that any ﬁrearms, ammunition, or explosives have been brought into the Cook
Islands in breach of this section or have been brought into the territorial waters
of the Cook Islands and are intended to be brought into the Cook Islands in
breach of this section, that constable or of f icer may seize the ﬁrearms,
ammunition, or explosives and detain the same. In any such case, such force
may be used as is reasonable and necessary for ef fecting the seizure of the
goods.
[Section 4: amended, on 7 June 1965, by section 3(4) of the Cook Islands Amendment Act 1965
(1965 No 1).]

5 Sale of Arms and Ammunition
(1) Except as hereinafter provided in this section, no person shall import into the
Cook Islands for the purposes of sale, or sell or expose or keep for sale in the
said islands, any ﬁrearms, or any cartridges, cartridge cases, gunpowder bullets,
shot or other ammunition.
(2) Any customs of f icer or constable may detain and examine the contents of any
package imported into the Cook Islands in respect whereof a breach of this
section is reasonably suspected.
(3) It shall be lawful for any person appointed for that purpose by the High
Commissioner to import into the Cook Islands, and to sell to any person, subject
to such conditions as may from time to time be imposed by the High
Commissioner) ﬁrearms and cartridges, cartridge cases, gunpowder, bullets,
shot and other ammunition. Every such person shall keep a correct and true
account of all ﬁrearms and ammunition imported or received by him, and of the
names of all persons to whom any ﬁrearms or ammunition are sold as aforesaid.
(4) If any constable or any of f icer of Customs has reasonable grounds to suspect
that any ﬁrearms or ammunition have been imported into the Cook Islands or
are exposed or kept for sale in breach of this section or have been brought into
the territorial waters of the Cook Islands and are intended to be imported into
the Cook Islands in breach of this section, that constable or of f icer may seize the
ﬁrearms or ammunition and detain the same. In any such case, such force may
be used as is reasonable and necessary for ef fecting the seizure of ﬁrearms or
ammunition.
[Section 5(2): amended, on 9 December 2017, by section 4 of the Arms Ordinance Amendment
Act 2017 (2017 No 20).]
[Section 5(3): amended, on 7 June 1965, by section 3(4) of the Cook Islands Amendment Act 1965
(1965 No 1).]
5A T raf ficking in ﬁrearms or ammunition
(1) No person may bring into the Cook Islands or the territorial waters of the Cook
Islands any ﬁrearm or ammunition that is intended for sale or supply outside the
Cook Islands, unless the person is authorised by the Minister of Police to
tranship the ﬁrearm or ammunition.
(2) Any customs of f icer or constable may detain and examine any package or
container brought into the Cook Islands or the territorial waters of the Cook
Islands if the of f icer or constable has reasonable grounds to suspect a breach of
subsection (1).
(3) A person who is authorised to tranship ﬁrearms or ammunition through the
Cook Islands or the territorial waters of the Cook Islands must provide to the
Minister of Police, before transhipment occurs, details of—
(a) the ﬁrearms or ammunition being transhipped; and
(b) where, when, and from whom it was obtained; and
(c) the intended destination of the ﬁrearms or ammunition.
(4) Any customs of f icer or constable who has reasonable grounds to suspect that
any ﬁrearms or ammunition have been brought into the Cook Islands or the
territorial waters of the Cook Islands in breach of subsection (1), or to suspect
that the details provided under subsection (3) are incorrect or incomplete, may
seize and detain the ﬁrearms or ammunition, using reasonable force if necessary .
[Section 5A: inserted, on 9 December 2017, by section 5 of the Arms Ordinance Amendment
Act 2017 (2017 No 20).]

6 Permit for purchase or supply of Firearms, etc
(1) No person shall, whether by way of purchase or in any other manner, procure
the possession of or supply any ﬁrearm, ammunition or explosive save in
pursuance of a permit issued to him in that behalf, by a person authorised in that
behalf by the High Commissioner.
(2) In proceedings for an of fence against this section the burden of proving the
existence and terms of any such permit as aforesaid shall lie on the accused.
(3) Every permit issued under this section shall remain in force for 6 months after
the date of the issue thereof, and shall then expire:
Provided that any such permit may from time to time be extended for periods
not longer than 1 month at any time,or may at any time during its currency be
revoked by the person who issued the permit or by the High Commissioner or
other person authorised by him.
[Section 6: amended, on 7 June 1965, by section 3(4) of the Cook Islands Amendment Act 1965
(1965 No 1).]
7 Possession of unregistered ﬁrearms prohibited
(1) On and after the commencement of this Ordinance no person shall be in
possession for a period longer than 7 days of any ﬁrearm unless he is registered
as the owner thereof under this Ordinance.
(2) No person other than a member of the Police and Defence Force (or, on an
island other than Rarotonga, the Chief Administration Of f icer) may possess a
pistol for longer than 7 days unless expressly authorised by the High
Commissioner.
(3) Any person authorised by the High Commissioner may require an owner to
produce any ﬁrearm for inspection at any time and refusal will constitute an
of fence under this section.
(4) In any prosecution for an of fence against this section, if the accused is proved
to have been in possession of the ﬁrearm, the burden of proving that he was
registered as aforesaid or that he had not been in possession thereof for more
than 7 days shall lie on the accused.
(5) Registration under this section shall be ef fected by obtaining a certiﬁcate of
registration from a person authorised in that behalf by the High Commissioner.
(6) Application for such a certiﬁcate must be made in writing, delivered at the
Police Station in Rarotonga or at the Senior Clerk’s of f ice in other islands and
setting out—
(a) the full name, abode and occupation of the applicant; and
(b) such description of the ﬁrearm as may , so far as practicable, enable it to
be identiﬁed.
(7) The person authorised to issue Certiﬁcates of Registration may require the
applicant to produce the ﬁrearm for his inspection and may withhold the
Certiﬁcate in the meantime.
(8) No person shall be exempt from the necessity of becoming registered as the
owner of a ﬁrearm under this section on the ground that he obtained that ﬁrearm
in pursuance of a permit issued under this Ordinance.
(9) If any person who is registered as the owner of any ﬁrearm is, in the opinion of
any person authorised to issue permits, not a ﬁt and proper person to be in
possession of the ﬁrearm, or any ﬁrearm in his possession is found unsafe, that

8 Unlawful acquisition of ﬁrearms etc
Every person commits an of fence against this Ordinance who procures, or
attempts to procure or conspires to procures, the possession to any ﬁrearms,
ammunition or explosive by way of trespass or otherwise without lawful right or
title thereto.
9 Presenting ﬁrearms at other persons
Every person commits an of fence against this Ordinance who, except for some
lawful and suf f icient purpose, presents a ﬁrearm, whether loaded or unloaded, at
any other person.
10 Arrest of of fenders
Any person reasonably suspected of having committed an of fence against this
Ordinance which is punishable by imprisonment be arrested by any constable
without warrant.
11 Search of suspected persons and seizure of ﬁrearms etc
(1) If any constable has reasonable grounds to suspect that any person being in a
public place is carrying or is in possession of any ﬁrearms, ammunition, or
explosive in breach of this Ordinance, that constable may , without warrant,
search that person, or any vehicle, package, or other thing there in his possession
or under his control, and may detain that person for the purpose of the search,
and may seize any such ﬁrearm, ammunition, or explosive and detain the same.
(2) If any constable has reasonable grounds to suspect that any person has in his
possession or under his control in any place any ﬁrearm, ammunition or
explosive and that that person is of unsound mind, or is in a state of intoxication,
or has attempted or threatened to kill or to do serious bodily injury to himself or
any other person, the constable may , without warrant, search that person or
place, and may detain that person for the purpose of the search, and may seize
any ﬁrearm, ammunition or explosive, and detain the same.
12 Search of land or buildings for ﬁrearms etc
If any constable has reasonable grounds to suspect that there is in any house,
building, land, vessel or other premises any ﬁrearms, ammunition, or explosives

13 Obstruction of Police
Every person commits an of fense against this Ordinance who obstructs any
constable in the exercise of any right of search, seizure, or detention conferred
by this Ordinance.
14 Disposal of article seized or surrendered
(1) All ﬁrearms, ammunition, or explosives seized or surrendered under this
Ordinance may be detained by the police for such period as the High
Commissioner thinks ﬁt, or may , in the discretion of the High Commissioner,
become the property of Her Majesty , free and discharged from all rights, title,
estate, or interest possessed in respect thereof by any other person.
(2) In any case where any such ﬁrearms, ammunition or explosives have become
the property of Her Majesty as aforesaid, the High Commissioner may pay
compensation out of the Cook Islands Treasury .
[Section 14: amended, on 7 June 1965, by section 3(4) of the Cook Islands Amendment Act 1965
(1965 No 1).]
15 Amount of compensation
The amount of compensation payable under any of the provisions of these
regulations in respect of any ﬁrearms, ammunition or explosives or other
property shall not in any case exceed the actual market value thereof, and in
case of dispute shall be determined by the High Court, whose decision shall be
ﬁnal.
16 Restoration of articles seized
On application, the Court may make such order as it thinks just and expedient
for the restoration of any ﬁrearms, ammunition or explosives seized and
detained in pursuance of the right of inspection, search, seizure or detention
conferred by this Ordinance not being ﬁrearms, ammunition or explosives
which have been forfeited or become the property of Her Majesty under the
provisions of this Ordinance.
17 Forfeitures
When any person is convicted of carrying, traf f icking, or being in possession of
any arms, ammunition or explosives in breach of this Ordinance, the Court may ,
as part of the conviction, order that the arms, ammunition or explosives shall be
forfeited and they shall be forfeited to the Crown accordingly , and may be
disposed of in such manner as the Court directs.
[Section 17: amended, on 9 December 2017, by section 7 of the Arms Ordinance Amendment
Act 2017 (2017 No 20).]

19 Use of ﬁrearms by youths
(1) No person shall sell or supply ﬁrearms or any kind of ammunition for a ﬁrearm
to any person under the age of 16 years.
(2) No person under the age of 16 years shall use or carry or have in his possession
any ﬁrearm or any kind of ammunition for a ﬁrearm.
(3) Every person who commits a breach of this section is liable to a ﬁne not
exceeding $1,000.
(4) Where any person under the age of 16 years is convicted of a breach of
subsection (2) this section, the ﬁrearm so used or carried by him or had in his
possession may , in the discretion of the High Court, be forfeited to the Crown
or be otherwise disposed of in such manner High Court directs.
(5) Where on the hearing of any proceedings the age of the person charged is not
proved, the High Court may decide on its own view and judgment whether that
person has attained the age of 16 years.
(6) No person under the age of 16 years shall be liable to imprisonment in respect
of any ﬁne imposed for a breach of subsection (2).
[Section 19(3): amended, on 9 December 2017, by section 8 of the Arms Ordinance Amendment

20 Carriage of loaded ﬁrearms on vehicles (including bicycles)
Except for police or defence purposes, or except in pursuance of a permit issued
to this ef fect, every person commits an of fence against this Ordinance who
operates any vehicle on which is carried any ﬁrearm loaded with a cartridge or
cartridges, whether in its breech, barrel, or magazine.
21 Liability of occupier of premises
For the purpose of this Ordinance every person in occupation of any land or
building on which any ﬁrearms, ammunition or explosives are found shall,
notwithstanding the liability of any other person, be deemed to be in the
possession of the ﬁrearms, ammunition, or explosives, unless he proves that
they were not his property and that they were in the possession of some other
person.
22 Notice of removal of ﬁrearms from Cook Islands
Every owner of a registered ﬁrearm who intends to remove the ﬁrearm out of the
Cook Islands shall give notice in writing of that intention to a constable not less
than 3 clear days before that removal, and shall surrender to that constable the
certiﬁcate of registration.
23 Notice of loss, theft, or destruction of registered ﬁrearms
Whenever a registered ﬁrearm is lost, stolen or destroyed, the owner of the
ﬁrearm shall forthwith give notice in writing of the loss, theft or destruction, as
the case may be, of the ﬁrearm to a constable and supply to that constable all
information in his possession respecting the loss, theft, or destruction as
aforesaid.

24 Production of certiﬁcates of registration and permits
Every person to whom any certiﬁcate or permit under this Ordinance has been
issued and who is authorised to retain the same shall, on demand of any
constable, produce that document forthwith for inspection and the ﬁrearm,
ammunition or explosive referred to therein.
25 Lost certiﬁcates and permits
Every certiﬁcate or permit issued under this Ordinance which has been lost or
destroyed, may , on proof of the loss or destruction to the satisfaction of any
person authorised to issue such a certiﬁcate or permit, be replaced by a new
certiﬁcate or permit on payment in the case of a certiﬁcate of the prescribed fee.
26 False statements
Every person who wilfully makes any false statement relating to any of the
particulars required to be known or registered under the provisions of this
Ordinance shall be liable to a ﬁne not exceeding £20.
27 General penalty for of fences
Every person who commits a breach of this Ordinance for which no penalty is
provided elsewhere than in this section is liable—
(a) in the case of an individual, to imprisonment for a term not exceeding
1 year, a ﬁne not exceeding $5,000, or to both:
(b) in any other case, to a ﬁne not exceeding $50,000.
[Section 27: amended, on 9 December 2017, by section 9 of the Arms Ordinance Amendment
Act 2017 (2017 No 20).]
28 Application of Ordinance to Of ficers of Defence Force or Police Force
Nothing in this Ordinance shall render unlawful the carriage or possession of
ﬁrearms, ammunition, or explosives belonging to the Crown and lawfully in the
possession of any member of the Defence Force or of the Police Force.
29 Fees
(1) The following fees shall be payable under this Act:
(a) for every original certiﬁcate of registration of a ﬁrearm — $10:
(b) for every original certiﬁcate of registration of a ﬁrearm issued in
replacement of a lost or destroyed certiﬁcate — $1.50.
(2) No such certiﬁcate shall be issued until the fee has been paid.
[Section 29: replaced, on 1 April 1977, by section 2 of the Arms Ordinance Amendment Act 1977
(1977 No 4).]
30 Application of registration fees and ﬁnes
All registration fees and ﬁnes levied and received under this Ordinance shall be
paid into the Cook Islands Treasury and shall form part of the revenues of the
Cook Islands.
31 Commencement
This Ordinance shall come into ef fect on 1 December 1954.
This Ordinance is administered by the Cook Islands Police.
