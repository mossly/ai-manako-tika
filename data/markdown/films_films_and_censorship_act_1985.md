4 Renter’s licence
(1) No person may rent or cause or permit to be rented, any ﬁlm unless he or she
is the holder of a renter’s licence.
(2) Any person who contravenes the provisions of subsection (1) is guilty of an
of fence and upon conviction is liable to a ﬁne not exceeding $5,000.
[Section 4(2): amended, on 18 April 2008, by section 7 of the Films and Censorship Amendment
Act 2008 (2008 No 6).]
5 Exhibitor’s licence
(1) No person may exhibit or cause or permit or suf fer to be exhibited any ﬁlm
unless he or she is the holder of an exhibitor’s licence.
(2) Any person who exhibits a ﬁlm in contravention of the provisions of
subsection (1) is guilty of an of fence and upon conviction is liable to a ﬁne not
exceeding $5,000.
[Section 5(2): amended, on 18 April 2008, by section 7 of the Films and Censorship Amendment
Act 2008 (2008 No 6).]
6 Duties of exhibitors
Every holder of an exhibitor’s licence must ensure that every exhibition is
conducted in such a manner as to provide for the safety , convenience and good
order of the persons attending thereat and that there is a responsible and
competent person operating the equipment used in the premises for the purpose
of the exhibition.
7 Exemptions
Films may be exhibited without an exhibitor’s licence—
(a) by any charitable, philanthropic, religious, educational, social or sporting
body or organisation where—
(i) the ﬁlm is exhibited to further the bona ﬁde objects or purpose of
the body or organisation, those objects or purposes being other
than the exhibiting of ﬁlm; and
(ii) admission to the exhibition is restricted to the members of that
body or organisation and their families; and
(iii) after meeting actual expenses and charges incurred in that
exhibition, any proceeds derived from the exhibition are applied
solely and entirely towards the funds of the body of organisation
so exhibiting the ﬁlm, and no portion of those proceeds is payable
to any person by way of fee, commission, or other ﬁnancial
reward for acts performed or services rendered by that person in
arranging, providing, or carrying out the exhibition except any fee
payable to any person projecting the ﬁlm:
(b) in any hotel premises which are the subject of a licence issued under the
provisions of the T ourist Authority Act 1968 where the exhibition is for
the beneﬁt of hotel guests only .
8 Applications
(1) Every application for a licence under this Act must be made to the Minister or
such of f icer as may be authorised pursuant to section 3 and must be made in
such form and manner and must contain, or be accompanied by , such
information, and documents as may be prescribed.

(1) Where—
(a) an applicant is refused a licence; or
(b) a licence is cancelled or suspended; or
(c) conditions imposed or attaching to a licence are varied,
the applicant or the licensee, as the case may be, may within 14 days appeal to
the High Court against such refusal, cancellation, suspension or variation.
(2) The decision of the High Court under this section is ﬁnal.
12 T ransfer of licenses
Every licence issued under this Act is only valid in respect of the premises for
which it is granted and is not transferable without the consent of the Minister or
a duly authorised of f icer.
13 Lost licence
Any licensee whose licence has been lost or destroyed may on payment of the
prescribed fee obtain a duplicate licence from the person by which the original
licence was issued.
14 Licences to be displayed
Every licensee must display and keep displayed in a prominent place at the
premises in respect of which a licence is issued, a current licence.

16 Films to be approved by censor before exhibition
No person may exhibit any ﬁlm other than a ﬁlm exempted under the provisions
of section 20 from the operation of this Part, unless and until such ﬁlm has been
approved by the censor for public exhibition.
17 Of fence to exhibit ﬁlm not approved by censor
(1) Every person who in any place to which the public are admitted, exhibits or is
concerned in the exhibition of any ﬁlm or part of a ﬁlm which—
(a) has not been approved by the censor; or
(b) is being shown in breach of any condition imposed by the censor,
commits an of fence and is liable to a ﬁne not exceeding $5,000 in respect of
every occasion on which such ﬁlm or any parts thereof have been so exhibited.
(2) Any ﬁlm in respect of which an of fence under this Act has been committed may
by order of the convicting court made in addition to any other penalty , be
forfeited and thereafter may be destroyed or otherwise dealt with as the Minister
may direct.

18 Duties of censors
(1) It is the duty of the censor to examine every ﬁlm duly submitted to him or her
for examination with a view to such ﬁlm being approved for purposes of
exhibition to the public.
(2) On the completion of the examination of any ﬁlm the censor may—
(a) approve the showing for exhibition:
(b) refuse to approve the same:
(c) agree to approve the same subject to such excisions from the ﬁlm he or
she thinks proper.

(3) The approval by the censor must not be given with respect to any ﬁlm or to any
part of a ﬁlm which in his or her opinion depicts any matter which is contrary
to public order or is indecent or the exhibition of which would for any other
reason be undesirable in the public interest.
(4) Such approval may be for general exhibition, or may be given subject to a
condition that the ﬁlm should be exhibited only to any speciﬁed class or classes
of persons, or may be given subject to any other conditions whatsoever that the
censor in the public interest thinks ﬁt to impose.
19 Fees
There is payable to the censor such fees in respect of the examination of ﬁlms

20 Exemption from censorship
(1) The censor, may on application made to him or her, waive compliance by any
person, organisation, or body , either unconditionally or subject to such
conditions as the censor thinks ﬁt, with the requirements of this Part in respect
of any ﬁlms of any of the following classes—
(a) ﬁlms used for educational and instructional purposes:
(b) ﬁlms directly related to the curriculum of primary , secondary or tertiary
educational institutions:
(c) ﬁlms wholly or mainly of a religious nature:
(d) ﬁlms that are wholly or mainly commercial advertisements relating to the
advertisers’ or sponsors’ activities:
(e) ﬁlms relating wholly or mainly to the social sciences, including
economics, geography , anthropology , and linguistics:
(f) ﬁlms depicting wholly or mainly cultural activities.
(2) The censor must not grant a waiver under subsection (1) in respect of any
particular ﬁlm or any particular class of ﬁlms if he or she has any reason to
believe that the exhibition of that ﬁlm or ﬁlms of that class is likely to be
injurious to the public good.
(3) The censor may at any time revoke any waiver granted under subsection (1),
either wholly or in part.
(4) Notwithstanding that the censor has granted a waiver to any person, body or
organisation in respect of any ﬁlm or class of ﬁlms under subsection (1) the
censor may require that person, body or organisation to submit that ﬁlm or any
particular ﬁlm of that class to him or her under this Part, in which case the
provisions of this Part apply accordingly .
21 Examination of ﬁlms by censor
In determining whether the exhibition of any ﬁlm is or is not likely to be
undesirable in the public interest, or is or is not contrary to public order, or is not
indecent, the censor must consider the following matters—
(a) the dominant ef fect of the ﬁlm as a whole:
(b) the extent to which the ﬁlm has merit, value or importance in relation to
artistic, social, cultural or other matters:
(c) the extent and degree to which, or the manner in which, the ﬁlm depicts,
includes, or treats anti-social behaviour, cruelty , violence, crime, sex, or
indecent or of fensive language or behaviour:
(d) the extent and degree to which and the manner in which the ﬁlm

23 Special provisions relating to ﬁlm sold or rented to public
(1) No person may sell to the public or rent to the public, or of fer or expose for sale

approved by a censor for sale or renting to the public, and any conditions
imposed by the censor in granting his or her approval are complied with.
(2) Subject to subsection (4), nothing in subsection (1) applies with respect to any
ﬁlm imported into the Cook Islands before the commencement of this section.
(3) Every person carrying on at the commencement of this section the business of
selling or renting ﬁlm to the public must, within 14 days after the
commencement of this section, supply to the censor a list specifying—
(a) the titles of all ﬁlm in his or her possession for sale or renting that were

(4) The censor may , by notice in writing to the person supplying such a list, direct
that he or she must not sell or rent or of fer or expose for sale or rent, any ﬁlm
speciﬁed in the list, unless it is approved by the censor for sale or renting and
is in compliance with any conditions imposed by the censor in granting that
approval.
(5) The foregoing provisions of this Part as far as they are applicable and with the
necessary modiﬁcations, apply with respect to every ﬁlm required to be
approved by the censor pursuant to this section as if the ﬁlm was ﬁlm intended

28 Functions of censor
The functions of the censor in relation to documents or sound recordings are—
(a) to determine the character of any document or sound recording submitted
to him or her for classiﬁcation:
(b) to classify documents or sound recordings submitted to him or her as
indecent or not indecent or as indecent in the hands of persons under a
speciﬁed age or as indecent unless their circulation is restricted to
speciﬁed persons or classes of persons or unless used for a particular
purpose, as the case may be:
(c) to hear and determine any questions relating to the character of a book or
sound recording referred to him or her by a court in any civil or criminal
proceedings whether under this Act or otherwise, and to forward a report
of its ﬁndings to that court:
(d) to determine whether a ﬁlm is a counterfeit or pirate copy produced
without or not bearing the appropriate authorisation of the copyright,
patent or authorised holder and therefore violating a copyright, patent or
a protection complementary certiﬁcate:
(e) to seize and destroy any such ﬁlm as described in paragraph (d).

29 Matters to be taken into account by censor
(1) In classifying or determining the character of any document or sound recording
the censor must take into consideration—
(a) the dominant ef fect of the document or sound recording:
(b) the person, classes of persons, or age groups to or among whom the
document or sound recording is or is intended or is likely to be published,
heard, distributed, sold, exhibited, played, given, sent, or delivered:
(c) the literary or artistic merit, or the medical, legal, political, social or
scientiﬁc character or importance of the document or sound recording:
(d) the price at which the book or sound recording sells or is intended to be
sold:
(e) whether any person is likely to be corrupted by reading the book or
hearing the sound recording and whether other persons are likely to
beneﬁt therefrom:

Pursuant to section 35 of the Films and Censorship Act 1985 the Queen’s Representative
acting by and with the advice and consent of the Executive council hereby makes the
following regulations.
1 Title
These regulations may be cited as the Films and censorship Regulations 1986.
2 Renter’s licence
Every application for a renter’s licence must be made in Form 1 in Schedule 1.
3 Exhibitor’s licence
Every application for an exhibitor’s licence must be made in Form 2 in
Schedule 1.
4 Licence
(1) Every application for a renter’s licence must be accompanied by a fee of $50.
(2) Every application for an exhibitor’s license must be accompanied by a fee of
$50.
(3) The fee for a duplicate of a licence is $50.
5 Licences
(1) A renter’s licence must be in Form 3 in Schedule 1.
(2) An exhibitor’s licence must be in Form 4 in Schedule 1.

Pursuant to sections 8, 19, and 31 of the Films and Censorship Act 1985, His Excellency
the Queen’s Representative acting by and with the advice and consent of the Executive
Council, hereby makes the following order.
1 Title and commencement
This order may be cited as the Films and Censorship (Fees) Order 1987 and
comes into force on 1 January 1988.
2 Licence fees
The fees payable annually to the censor for the issue of a renter’s licence and for
an exhibitor’s licence are as set out in the Schedule.
3 Examination fees
The fees payable to the censor for the examination by the censor of video tapes,
cinematographic ﬁlm, documents and sound tapes are as set out in the Schedule.
