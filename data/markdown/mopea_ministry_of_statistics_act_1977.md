Part 3
Statistics Division
9 Functions of Statistics Division
(1) The functions of the Statistics Division are to collect, compile, analyse, abstract
and publish statistics with or without observations thereon, for the purpose of
furnishing information required in the formation or carrying out of Government
policy in any ﬁeld or otherwise required for Government purposes or for
meeting the needs of trade, commerce, industry or agriculture.
(2) The Statistics Division is responsible for administering the Statistics Act 1966.
10 Statistics Of ficer
(1) There must from time to time be appointed as a member of the Public Service
a Statistics Of f icer who is the administrative head of the Statistics Division and
responsible to the Secretary for the Division’s operations.
(2) There must from time to time be appointed as a member of the Public Service
a Deputy Statistics Of f icer who must act as Statistics Of f icer in the case of
absence from duty of the Statistics Of f icer (from whatever cause arising) or on
the occurrence from any cause of a vacancy in the position of Statistics Of f icer
(whether by reason of death, resignation or otherwise) so long as such absence
or vacancy continues.
(3) The Deputy Statistics Of f icer, while acting as Statistics Of f icer, must exercise
the duties and has the powers of the Statistics Of f icer, and the fact that he or she
so acts is suf f icient evidence of his or her authority to do so.
11 Other of ficers and employees
(1) There may from time to time be appointed as members of the Public Service
such other of f icers and employees of the Statistics Division as may be required.
(2) All of f icers and employees must act under the direction of the Statistics Of f icer
in the exercise and performance of the powers, duties and functions conferred or
imposed on them by any enactment.
(3) All persons (except those elsewhere in this Act excluded) who at the
commencement of this Act are of f icers and employees of the Statistics Of f ice are
deemed to have been appointed to the Statistics Division under this Act.

Part 5
Other provisions
15 Annual reports
(1) The Secretary must as soon as practicable after the end of each ﬁnancial year
furnish to the Minister a report on the operation of Ministry as well as a report
on the Cook Islands economy for that year.
(2) A copy of each report must be laid before Parliament within 28 days after it has
been furnished to the Minister if Parliament is then in session, and, if not, must
be laid before Parliament within 28 days after the commencement of the next
ensuing session.
16 Regulations
(1) The Queen’s Representative, by Order in Executive Council, may from time to
time make all such regulations as are deemed necessary or expedient for the
purpose of giving full ef fect to this Act and for the due administration thereof.
(2) All regulations made under this section must be laid before Parliament by the
Minister within 28 days of the date of their making if Parliament is then in
session, and, if not, must be laid before Parliament within 28 days after the date
of commencement of the next ensuing session.
[Section 16(1): amended, on 6 April 1982, by section 4(3) of the Constitution Amendment (No 10)
Act 1981–82 (1981–82 No 20).]
