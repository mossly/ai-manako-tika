An Act to provide for the management of solid and hazardous waste.
1 Title
This Act is the Solid and Hazardous W aste Act 2025.
2 Commencement
This Act comes into force on 1 July 2025.
Part 1
Preliminary matters
3 Purpose
The purposes of this Act are to—
(a) establish rules for the responsible disposal of dif ferent types of waste:
(b) provide for the collection of waste in the Cook Islands:
(c) provide for the establishment and operation of waste facilities to which
waste can be taken for disposal:
(d) ban or control the importation of certain articles and substances that are,
or that generate, unwanted waste:
(e) establish a scheme for importers of prescribed goods to pay an advance
recovery and disposal fee:
(f) prohibit retailers from supplying plastic shopping bags for the purpose of
enabling goods sold by the retailers to be delivered to or carried by
customers:
(g) enable the Cook Islands to comply with relevant international
conventions regarding the disposal of solid and hazardous waste:
(h) provide appropriate mechanisms for enforcement.

Part 3

Domestic waste and recyclable waste
12 Disposing of domestic waste and recyclable waste
(1) No person may dispose or attempt to dispose of domestic waste or recyclable
waste by—
(a) burning it; or
(b) burying it; or
(c) dumping it on any land or in any water; or
(d) attempting to do any of the things in paragraphs (a) to (c).
(2) A person who contravenes subsection (1) commits an of fence and is liable on
conviction to a ﬁne not exceeding $10,000.
(3) It is a defence to a charge under this section if the person charged proves that the
waste was litter that was deposited in a receptacle as required under Part 4.
(4) This section does not apply to the disposal of domestic waste or recyclable
waste at a waste facility .
Bulky waste, building and demolition waste, and organic waste
13 Disposing of bulky waste
(1) No person may dispose or attempt to dispose of bulky waste by burning it or
attempting to burn it.
(2) Subject to subsection (3) and section 14, no person may bury or dump bulky
waste, or attempt to do so,—
(a) in or on a public place; or
(b) in or on private land; or
(c) in water.
(3) A vehicle or other machinery that is not working or does not have a current
warrant of ﬁtness may be stored on private land, but the occupier of the land
must ensure that it is—
(a) stored in a building (for example, a garage); or
(b) disassembled for parts promptly (and no later than 2 months after storage
on the land begins) and the remains taken to a waste facility without
delay .
(4) A person who wishes to dispose of bulky waste must deliver it to a waste facility
that accepts bulky waste or use a commercial operator to do so ( see Part 5).
(5) A person commits an of fence and is liable on conviction to a ﬁne not exceeding
$10,000 if they—
(a) contravene subsection (1); or
(b) contravene subsection (2); or
(c) fail to comply with the requirements in subsection (3) when storing a
vehicle or other machinery , unless section 48(2) applies.
(6) This section does not apply to the disposal of bulky waste at a waste facility .

Hazardous waste
16 Disposal of hazardous waste, etc
(1) No person may dispose or attempt to dispose of hazardous waste—

(b) by burying or dumping it on or in land; or

(ii) putting it out for collection (if hazardous waste is collected by a
Ministry or an Island Government).
(4) A person commits an of fence if they—
(a) contravene subsection (1); or
(b) dispose or attempt to dispose of hazardous waste other than in accordance
with subsection (2) or (3).
