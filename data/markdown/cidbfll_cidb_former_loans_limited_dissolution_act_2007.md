An Act to dissolve CIDB Former Loans Limited and to transfer its assets to Bank of the
Cook Islands Limited
BE IT ENACTED by the Parliament of the Cook Islands in Session assembled, and by
the authority of the same as follows:
1 Short title and commencement
(1) This Act may be cited as the CIDB Former Loans Limited Dissolution Act 2007.
(2) This Act comes into force on 30 June 2007.
2 Interpretation
In this Act—
Bank means Bank of the Cook Islands Limited
Companies Act means the Companies Act 1955 of New Zealand as applied in
the Cook Islands by the Companies Act 1970–71
Company means the company incorporated and registered at A varua under the
name “CIDB Former Loans Limited”.
3 Dissolution
(1) Upon the coming into force of this Act—
(a) all rights, interests, liabilities and obligations of the Company are deemed
to have been assigned and transferred to, and belong to and vest in the
Bank; and
(b) all real and personal property (including choses in action) of the
Company is deemed to have been assigned and transferred to, and belong
to and vest in the Bank; and
(c) the name of the Bank is deemed to have been substituted for the name of
the Company on every register, document or instrument of estate or
interest; and
(d) the Registrar of Companies must, despite any other provision of the
Companies Act, strike the name of the Company from the Register kept
by the Registrar pursuant to section 7 of that Act and the Company is
thereupon deemed to have been dissolved.
(2) The value of all property assigned and transferred pursuant to subsection (1) is
the value agreed between the respective boards of directors of the Bank and of
the Company and failing such agreement within 1 month of the coming into
force of this Act is as determined by the Bank’s auditors.
(3) Despite anything to the contrary in the Income T ax Act 1997, the gross assets
including any tax losses vested by this Act in the Bank are deemed to have been
so vested as a going concern.
(4) The Company is not required, and has never been required, to comply with the
provisions of section 36 of the Bank of the Cook Islands Act 2003.
(5) Despite anything contained in the Leases Restrictions Act 1976, the approval of
the Leases Approval Tribunal is not required to any assignment of lease,
sublease or mortgage deemed by this Act to have occurred.
