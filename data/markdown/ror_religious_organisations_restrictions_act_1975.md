An Act to restrict the establishment of religious organisations in the Cook Islands.
BE IT ENACTED by the Legislative Assembly of the Cook Islands in Session
assembled, and by authority of the same, as follows—
1 Short title
This Act may be cited as the Religious Organisations Restrictions Act 1975.
2 Interpretation
In this Act, unless the context otherwise requires—
Minister means the Minister charged with the responsibility of Justice.
3 Restrictions on establishment of religious organisations
It is hereby declared that except with the prior approval of the Minister and upon
and subject to such conditions as he or she may impose there must not be
established in the Cook Islands any religious organisation or association of
persons (whether incorporated or not) other than organisations or associations of
or representing the following—
(a) the Cook Islands Christian Church:
(b) the Roman Catholic Church:
(c) the Seventh-day Adventist Church:
(d) the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints:
Provided that the Minister must not withhold approval for the establishment of
any religious organisation unless satisﬁed that such establishment would be
contrary to local customs and practices, public safety , order or morals, or the
general welfare or the security of the Cook Islands.
[Section 3: amended, on 2 December 1994, by section 2 of the Religious Organisations Restrictions
Amendment Act 1994 (1994 No 23) .]
4 Restrictions on use of buildings
(1) No religious organisation or association of persons (whether incorporated or
not) other than—
(a) the Cook Islands Christian Church:
(b) the Roman Catholic Church:
(c) the Seventh-day Adventist Church:
(d) the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints:
(e) such other organisation or association of persons as may be approved by
the Minister:
may establish or use any church, hall, house, tent or other building for the
purpose of any public meeting or place of worship to which the public has
access.
(2) If any church, hall, house, tent or building is established or used in
contravention of the provisions of subsection (1) the said church, hall, house,
tent or building may by order of the High Court be ordered to be closed or
demolished.
(3) The closure or demolition or any such church, hall, house, tent or building must
be carried out as directed by the Court and the costs or such closure or
demolition must be paid as ordered by the Court.

5 Restrictions on meeting in or within view of public place
(1) No religious organisation or association of persons (whether incorporated or
not) other than—
(a) the Cook Islands Christian Church:
(b) the Roman Catholic Church:
(c) the Seventh-day Adventist Church:
(d) the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints:
(e) such other organisation or association of persons as may be approved by
the Minister:
may conduct any meeting for a religious purpose in any place to which the
public has access or in view of any such place.
(2) Any person who conducts any meeting in contravention of the provisions of
subsection (1) commits an of fence and is liable upon conviction to a ﬁne not
exceeding $200.
This Act is administered by the Justice Department.
Published under the authority of the Parliament of the Cook Islands.
