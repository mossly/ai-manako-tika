An Act to provide for the appointment of Notaries Public.
BE IT ENACTED by the Parliament of the Cook Islands in Session assembled and by
the authority of the same as follows:
1 Short title and commencement
(1) This Act may be cited as the Notaries Public Act 1992.
(2) This Act comes into force on a day to be appointed by the Minister and notiﬁed
by publication in the Gazette.
2 Interpretation
In this Act, unless the context otherwise requires—
Chief Justice means the Chief Justice of the High Court of the Cook Islands
Court means the High Court of the Cook Islands
Judge means a Judge of the High Court of the Cook Islands
Registrar means the Registrar of the High Court of the Cook Islands.
3 Appointment of Notaries Public
The Chief Justice may , by instrument under his or her hand, appoint any
barrister or solicitor practising in the Cook Islands and who has demonstrated to
the satisfaction of the Chief Justice that he or she has a knowledge and
understanding of the functions and duties of a Notary Public adequate to enable
him or her to carry out those functions and duties to be a Notary Public for the
Cook Islands to discharge the duties assigned to such of f ice whether by the laws
in force in the Cook Islands, or by the practise of commerce.
4 Notaries Public to be sworn and enrolled
(1) Every person appointed to be a Notary Public pursuant to this Act must, before
entering upon the duties of his or her of f ice—
(a) take the oath as hereinafter provided; and
(b) in the case of a person other than a person employed by the Government
of the Cook Islands or any of its agencies or instrumentalities, pay to the
Registrar a fee of $200; and
(c) whereupon the Registrar must enrol his or her name in a book to be called
the “Roll of Notaries Public” which must be kept for that purpose in the
of f ice of the Registrar.
(2) Every person so enrolled is entitled to a certiﬁcate of enrolment under seal of the
High Court in the form speciﬁed in Schedule 2, and save as provided in
section 7 no person whose name is not enrolled is entitled to perform the duties
of a Notary Public within the Cook Islands.
(3) The oath to be sworn on appointment by a Notary Public is—
“I, . . . . . . . . . . . . do swear that I will faithfully exercise the of f ice of a Notary
Public; I will faithfully make contracts or instruments for or between any party or
parties requiring the same, and I will not add or diminish any thing without the
knowledge or consent of such party or parties that may alter the substance of the
fact; I will not make or attest any act, contract or instrument in which I shall know
there is violence or fraud; and in all things I will act uprightly and justly in the

5 Notaries Public to be of ficers
Every person discharging the duties of a Notary Public is deemed to be an
of f icer of the Court.
6 Revocation of appointments
(1) The Chief Justice may revoke the appointment made under section 3 of any
Notary Public who is guilty of fraudulent or improper conduct in the discharge
of their duties as a Notary Public, or who makes any charge or demand any
payment for any notarial acts in excess of those prescribed.
(2) The Registrar must remove from the Roll of Notaries Public the name of any
person whose appointment has been revoked under the provisions of
subsection (1) and the certiﬁcate of enrolment of such person must be delivered
up to the Registrar who must cancel it.
7 Saving existing Notaries Public
It is lawful for any person who before the commencement of this Act lawfully
carried out the duties of a Notary Public within the Cook Islands to continue to
carry out the duties of a Notary Public within the Cook Islands and any such
person is not required to comply with the requirements of section 3.
8 Penalty for unlawfully practising as Notary Public
Except as provided in section 4 any person who holds himself or herself out to
be a Notary Public or who receives any fee or reward as a Notary Public, and
who at the time is not the holder of a certiﬁcate of enrolment then in force, is
guilty of an of fence and liable to a ﬁne not exceeding $500.
9 Notary Public to refuse to act in suspicious circumstances
(1) A Notary Public must refuse to act in any case where it appears to him or her
that the circumstances are suspicious and do not warrant the protest or other
notarial act demanded.
(2) Any person who considers himself or herself aggrieved by the refusal of a
Notary Public to note the protest or to do any other notarial act demanded may
apply to a Judge for an order calling upon the Notary Public to act in the
execution of his or her of f ice.
(3) Before applying for such an order the person wishing so to apply must cause
reasonable notice of the application to be given to the Notary Public refusing to
act and to such persons in the Cook Islands (if any) as may be interested in the
subject of the protest or other notarial act demanded.
10 Notary Public to mark refusal of document
Whenever a Notary Public refuses to note the protest or to do any notarial act
demanded of him, the Notary Public must endorse the logbook, bill of exchange
or other document with a note of his or her refusal, signed and dated by him or
her.

11 Penalty for false certiﬁcate, etc
Any Notary Public or other person who wilfully certiﬁes or propounds any false
statement or document or who fraudulently with intent to deceive conceals,
withholds or perverts any fact or document pertinent to the subject of protest or
other notarial act is guilty of an of fence and is liable to imprisonment for a term
not exceeding 2 years.

13 Fees

those speciﬁed in Schedule 1.
(2) The fees set out in Schedule 1 may from time to time be amended by Order in
Executive Council.
