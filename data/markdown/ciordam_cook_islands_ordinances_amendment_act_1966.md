An Act to provide for the Administration of Ordinances of the Cook Islands by Ministers.
BE IT ENACTED by the Legislative Assembly of the Cook Islands in Session
assembled, and by the authority of the same, as follows:
1 Short title
This Act may be cited as the Cook Islands Ordinances Amendment Act 1966.
2 Interpretation
the Constitution means the Constitution of the Cook Islands (as set out in
Schedule 2 to the Cook Islands Amendment Act 1965 of the Parliament of New
Zealand)
Minister means a Minister appointed under Article 13 of the Constitution
Queen’s Representative means the Queen’s Representative appointed under
Article 3 of the Constitution.
[Section 2 High Commissioner: repealed, on 6 April 1982, by section 4(3) of the Constitution
Amendment (No 10) Act 1981–82 (1981–82 No 20).]
[Section 2 Queen’s Representative: amended, on 6 April 1982, by section 4(3) of the Constitution
Amendment (No 10) Act 1981–82 (1981–82 No 20).]
3 Ministers to administer ordinances
(1) In any Ordinance of the Cook Islands where any thing, act, consent, duty ,
power, or function is required to be done, performed, exercised, or forborne by
the High Commissioner the same must be done, performed, exercised, or
forborne by the appropriate Minister to the same intent and ef fect in all respects
as if when the Ordinance was enacted the Minister had been so authorised in
lieu of the High Commissioner.
(2) The appropriate Minister referred to in the preceding subsection is the Minister
charged with administering the Ordinance or Ordinances set forth in the
Schedule.
4 Additions to Schedule by Order in Council
The Queen’s Representative may from time to time by Order in Executive
Council add to the list of Ordinances to be administered by the appropriate
Minister as set out in the Schedule, and thereafter such additional Ordinances
must be administered by such appropriate Minister to the same ef fect as if
inserted at the date of commencement of this Act.
[Section 4: amended, on 6 April 1982, by section 4(3) of the Constitution Amendment (No 10) Act
1981–82 (1981–82 No 20).]
5 Consequential amendment
Section 57(3) of the Cook Islands Amendment Act 1964 is hereby amended by
deleting the words “High Commissioner” in any Ordinance of the Cook Islands
where they conﬂict with the provisions of this Act and substituting the word
“Minister”.
