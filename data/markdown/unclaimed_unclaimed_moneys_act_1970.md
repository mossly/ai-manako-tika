An Act to provide for the disposition of certain unclaimed moneys.
BE IT ENACTED by the Legislative Assembly of the Cook Islands in Session
assembled, and by the authority of the same, as follows:
1 Short title
This Act may be cited as the Unclaimed Moneys Act 1970.

3 Act to bind the Crown
This Act shall bind the Crown.
4 Deemed unclaimed moneys
(1) The following moneys deposited in any bank become unclaimed moneys at the
times subsequently mentioned (whether or not they have at any time theretofore
become payable) namely:
(a) moneys deposited so as to bear interest for a ﬁxed term, at the expiration
of 6 years from the date when such ﬁxed term expired:
(b) moneys deposited so as to bear interest without any limitation of time, at
the expiration of 25 years from the date when the account was last
operated on by the customer:

(c) moneys deposited upon current account or otherwise and not bearing
interest, at the expiration of 6 years from the date when the account was
last operated on by the customer in the case of a bank other than a
Savings Bank, and at the expiration of 25 years from such date in the case
of a Savings Bank.
(2) The proceeds of any life insurance policy become unclaimed moneys on the
expiration of 6 years after the date on which the company ﬁrst has reason to
suppose that the policy has matured by death, or from the date (if any) on which
the policy matures otherwise than by death (whichever date is the earlier),
whether such death has been legally proved or not, and despite that by the terms
of the policy , the proceeds thereof are not payable except on proof of death, or
on proof of age or any other collateral matter.
(3) Any money in any account with any person, partnership company or corporation
which has not been operated upon for 6 years is deemed to be unclaimed
moneys.
(4) Public moneys to which sections 41 and 42 of the Public Moneys Act 1969
apply become unclaimed moneys within the meaning of this Act at the
expiration of 1 year after having become payable to the depositor thereof or
other person entitled thereto, or at the expiration of such longer period as the
Financial Secretary in any case may direct.
5 Register to be kept
It is the duty of every holder on the ﬁrst day of June in each year, to enter in a
register, to be kept by such holder at its head or principal of f ice in the Cook
Islands, in the form in the Schedule (or to the ef fect thereof and with the
particulars therein speciﬁed,) all unclaimed moneys in the Cook Islands, and
from and after the eighth day of June in each year such register must be open for
the inspection of all persons at such head or principal of f ices during ordinary
business hours, on payment of a fee of 10 cents:
Provided that on ceasing to carry on business in the Cook Islands a holder must
deposit its register in the custody of the Registrar of the High Court nearest to
that place where such register was theretofore kept:
Provided further that any holder may at any time so deposit any book or part of
its register in which no entry has been made for a period of not less than 6 years
immediately preceding the date of such deposit.
6 Notiﬁcation to Minister and persons af fected
Not later than the 30th day of June in each year, or such later day as the Minister
approves in writing, every holder must furnish to the Minister a copy of every
entry made on the ﬁrst day of that month in the register kept by it pursuant to
section 5 and must post to every person in respect of whom any such entry was
made as the owner of unclaimed money by letter addressed to such person at his
or her last known place of business or abode, a notice specifying the amount of
such money and the fact that it is entered in such register as unclaimed money .
7 Of fences
If default is made in complying with section 5 or 6, the holder, and every
director, manager, secretary , or other of f icer of the holder who knowingly and
wilfully authorises and permits the default, is liable to a ﬁne of $5 for every day
during which the default continues, with a maximum ﬁne of $200.

8 Payment of unclaimed moneys to Public Account
(1) All unclaimed moneys which have not been paid by a holder to the owner
thereof within 3 months after the posting to such owner of the notice prescribed
by section 6 hereof must be paid by the holder into the Public Account, and the
holder is then relieved from all further liability in respect of the moneys so paid.
(2) All moneys payable to the credit of the Public Account in accordance with this
section are recoverable in any court of competent jurisdiction as a debt due to
the owner.
9 Examination of accounts
(1) The Minister or Financial Secretary may at any time examine any register kept
by a holder pursuant to section 5 and any account of the holder relating to
unclaimed moneys, and may for that purpose require the production before him
or her or any of f icer of the Treasury of any book, vouchers, or documents
relating to such moneys, and if any error is found in such register or accounts or
in any copy of an entry in such register furnished to the Financial Secretary or
in any notice sent to any person, the Financial Secretary may require the same
to be amended.
(2) The Financial Secretary or any of f icer of the Treasury authorised by him or her
in that behalf shall at all times have full and free access to all books and
documents for the purposes of this Act, and for this purpose may make extracts
from or copies of any such books or documents.
(3) Every person must from time to time as required by the Financial Secretary
furnish in writing any information or produce any books or documents which
may be within his or her knowledge, or in his or her possession or control, as the
case may be, and which relate to moneys which are or may be unclaimed
moneys.
(4) The Financial Secretary and every person authorised by him or her as aforesaid,
must maintain and aid in maintaining the secrecy of all matters which come to
his or her knowledge in the course of any such inspection as aforesaid and must
not communicate any such matters to any person, except for the purpose of
carrying this Act into ef fect.
(5) Every person who fails to comply with any of the requirements of this section
commits an of fence, and is liable on conviction to a ﬁne not exceeding $200.
10 Payment to claimant
(1) If any claimant makes any demand against the Minister for any money paid to
him or her, the Minister, on being satisﬁed that the claimant is the owner of the
money demanded by him or her, must order and direct payment thereof to be
made to him or her and the same is statutory expenditure and not require any
further speciﬁc appropriation.
(2) The Minister may from time to time, either generally or particularly , by writing
under his or her hand delegate to the Financial Secretary the power conferred on
the Minister by this section to pay any claimant any money paid into the Public
Account under this Act.
(3) Where the Financial Secretary purports to act pursuant to any delegation under
this section, he or she is, in the absence of proof to the contrary , presumed to be
acting in accordance with the terms of the delegation.

11 Responsibility of Minister
Where any unclaimed moneys paid to any claimant are afterwards claimed by
any other person, the Minister or the Financial Secretary is not responsible for
the payment of the same, but such person may have recourse against the
claimant to whom the Minister or the Financial Secretary has paid the
unclaimed moneys.
